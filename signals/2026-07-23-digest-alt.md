## Macro / Structural Lens

### Primary Editorial Candidates

#### 1. China's deflation trap: stimulus without demand
- **Topic title:** China's deflation trap: stimulus without demand
- **Why this matters now:** China's full-year 2025 CPI was 0%, the lowest in 16 years. December 2025 CPI rose to 0.8% YoY, but the increase was driven by food prices (fresh vegetables +18.2%, beef +6.9%) while pork fell 14.6% and core demand remained weak. Beijing has signaled modest stimulus but has not announced a large consumption-led package.
- **Primary asset classes affected:** Chinese equities, property, commodities, EM FX, global manufacturing margins.
- **What makes this structurally interesting:** China is running a stimulus pedal without a consumption engine. Trade-in subsidies are supply-side; the missing piece is household income confidence and property wealth effects. This is a balance-sheet recession dressed in industrial policy.
- **What most commentary is missing:** The composition of inflation. Headline CPI is not recovering because demand is recovering; it is recovering because food supply shocks are temporary. Core inflation and property prices are still the real signal.
- **Editorial wedge:** Narrative Deconstruction / Market Mechanics
- **Sources:**
  - [Terra BKK — China's inflation in 2025 stands at 0%](https://www.terrabkk.com/en/articles/209841/)
  - [MarketScreener — China likely to chase 5% GDP growth in 2026](https://www.marketscreener.com/news/china-likely-to-chase-5-gdp-growth-in-2026-in-bid-to-end-deflation-ce7d51dedb8df725)
  - [Davis Vanguard — China property bubble burst](https://davisvanguard.org/2026/07/china-property-bubble-burst/)

#### 2. AI data centers are hitting the power-semiconductor wall
- **Topic title:** AI data centers are hitting the power-semiconductor wall
- **Why this matters now:** A power IC shortage is expected throughout 2026, driven by AI data center server demand. NVIDIA reported Q4 FY2026 data center revenue of $62.3 billion (+75% YoY) and full-year FY2026 data center revenue of $197.3 billion. Data center infrastructure management is projected to grow from $3.47 billion in 2025 to $13.98 billion by 2033 (19.4% CAGR).
- **Primary asset classes affected:** Semiconductors, power management chips, data center REITs, utilities, copper, cooling/water infrastructure.
- **What makes this structurally interesting:** The AI bottleneck has moved from chip design to advanced packaging, HBM memory, and electrical power. Power semiconductors have evolved from background components into strategic enablers.
- **What most commentary is missing:** The physical infrastructure lag. Building GPU clusters is fast; building the power conversion, cooling, and grid interconnection is slow. The marginal AI dollar is increasingly an infrastructure dollar, not a chip dollar.
- **Editorial wedge:** Market Mechanics / Long-cycle Structural
- **Sources:**
  - [Accuris Tech — AI data centers reshaping electronic component supply](https://accuristech.com/blog/ai-data-center-electronic-component-supply/)
  - [DataM Intelligence — Semiconductor investment outlook 2026](https://www.datamintelligence.com/blogs/semiconductor-investment-outlook-2026)

#### 3. Copper and gold are pricing two different worlds
- **Topic title:** Copper and gold are pricing two different worlds
- **Why this matters now:** Gold and copper have both been strong, but for different reasons. Gold is supported by central bank buying, fiscal deficits, and geopolitical hedging. Copper is supported by supply tightness and energy-transition demand, but was in surplus in 2025 and faces tariff-driven demand headwinds. JPMorgan projected copper could peak around $12,500/metric ton in Q2 2026; S&P Global says copper's 2026 average price forecast remains just above $12,000/t.
- **Primary asset classes affected:** Mining equities, industrial metals, precious metals, EM current accounts, energy-transition capital goods.
- **What makes this structurally interesting:** Gold is a monetary/story metal; copper is a physical/demand metal. When both rally, it usually means either the world is reindustrializing or the monetary system is being questioned. Right now they may be telling different stories.
- **What most commentary is missing:** The tension between copper supply tightness and demand uncertainty. AI/data center demand is new and structural; tariff drag and EV slowdown are cyclical and policy-driven. The net direction depends on which force wins.
- **Editorial wedge:** Market Deviations / Narrative Deconstruction
- **Sources:**
  - [S&P Global — Copper and gold market outlook 2026](https://www.spglobal.com/market-intelligence/en/news-insights/research/2026/04/copper-gold-market-outlook-2026-prices-supply-mining-costs)

#### 4. Japan's carry trade is approaching a structural tipping point
- **Topic title:** Japan's carry trade is approaching a structural tipping point
- **Why this matters now:** The BoJ raised its policy rate to 1.0% on 16 June 2026, the highest since 1995, in a 7–1 vote and signaled further tightening. The Fed-BoJ spread has compressed from much wider levels to +2.62% now and is projected at +1.38% in 12 months. The yen carry trade has been a persistent capital-flow architecture for decades.
- **Primary asset classes affected:** Yen, JGBs, global carry trades, EM equities/FX, Japanese equities, volatility.
- **What makes this structurally interesting:** This is the first sustained BoJ hiking cycle in a generation. The unwind of yen-funded positions is not a one-day event; it is a slow structural repricing of global risk premiums.
- **What most commentary is missing:** The feedback loop. Higher BoJ rates strengthen the yen, which pressures carry-trade positions, which forces more unwinding, which strengthens the yen further. The tipping point is not a rate level; it is a positioning level.
- **Editorial wedge:** Market Mechanics / Long-cycle Structural
- **Sources:**
  - [NewTrading.io — Japan interest rate 2026](https://www.newtrading.io/japan-interest-rate/)
  - [PipTheory — Japan CPI preview June 2026](https://www.piptheory.com/research/japan-cpi-june-2026-preview-yen-jpy)
