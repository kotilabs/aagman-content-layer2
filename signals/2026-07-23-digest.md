### Primary Editorial Candidates

#### 1. Strait of Hormuz: the chokepoint market nobody can price
- **Topic title:** Strait of Hormuz: the chokepoint market nobody can price
- **Why this matters now:** Active US-Iran hostilities have collapsed tanker transits through the Strait of Hormuz to roughly 10–15 vessels per day versus a pre-crisis baseline near 88 per day. Brent crude has moved from the high-$70s to above $94 inside a week as the ceasefire collapsed and retaliatory strikes resumed.
- **Primary asset classes affected:** Crude oil, LNG, refining margins, energy equities, Indian current account / rupee, inflation-linked bonds.
- **What makes this structurally interesting:** This is the largest volumetric oil supply shock in market history by some estimates — roughly 10 mn b/d of accessible supply removed during the closure. Yet prices have not gone vertical because the market is pricing a reopening path. The tension is between the physical chokepoint and the political settlement.
- **What most commentary is missing:** The dual-corridor risk. A Hormuz closure plus Red Sea disruption would leave Asia short of Atlantic-barrel alternatives. Also, the Saudi East-West pipeline can only bypass ~5 mn b/d of the ~20 mn b/d that normally transits Hormuz.
- **Editorial wedge:** Market Mechanics / Narrative Deconstruction

#### 2. FII exodus, DII absorption: India's capital-flow regime change
- **Topic title:** FII exodus, DII absorption: India's capital-flow regime change
- **Why this matters now:** FIIs have sold Indian equities for ten consecutive months through April 2026 (~₹70,100 cr in April alone), while DIIs have bought ~₹4.16 lakh crore in 2026 year-to-early-June. The market has stopped falling because domestic flows have replaced foreign flows.
- **Primary asset classes affected:** Indian equities, rupee, sector leadership, large-cap vs mid-cap, mutual fund flows.
- **What makes this structurally interesting:** This is not an India-only story. It is a relative allocation decision — money is leaving EM broadly and rotating toward North Asian tech/AI trades. The domestic bid has compressed volatility and created a divergence between rupee-denominated and dollar-denominated returns.
- **What most commentary is missing:** The dollar-return math. Nifty is down ~8% from its September 2024 peak in rupee terms but ~19% in dollar terms. Foreign investors are not experiencing stability; they are experiencing a grinding drawdown delivered through the currency channel.
- **Editorial wedge:** Market Deviations / Long-cycle Structural
- **Sources:**

#### 3. Fed's hawkish turn: from cuts to hikes
- **Topic title:** Fed's hawkish turn: from cuts to hikes
- **Why this matters now:** The June 2026 FOMC meeting under Chair Kevin Warsh held rates at 3.50%–3.75% but revised year-end PCE inflation up to 3.6% from 2.7%. Nine of eighteen policymakers now expect at least one rate hike in 2026. Markets had priced cuts.
- **Primary asset classes affected:** US Treasuries, USD, equities, gold, Bitcoin, EM capital flows.
- **What makes this structurally interesting:** The Fed is being forced to acknowledge that energy price shocks and AI infrastructure demand are sustaining inflation. This is a regime shift from the 2025 easing narrative.
- **What most commentary is missing:** The divergence between Fed dots and market pricing. The longer this gap persists without either side capitulating, the more violent the repricing when it resolves.
- **Editorial wedge:** Narrative Deconstruction / Market Deviations
- **Sources:**
