You're writing copy for a standalone social carousel. The voice is Financial Times leader column meets Matt Levine: dry, confident, structurally argued, lightly literary. The reader is smart and busy. You are walking them through a complete argument, not selling them a feeling.

Source: the research artifact for this signal.

The job: Decide how many slides the argument actually needs (usually 8–12). Each slide advances the argument by one beat. Open with a hook that names the shift, not the topic. Close with the open question the argument leaves hanging. The full story must resolve within the carousel — there is no external link or "read more."

Voice rules:

Lead with the concrete, follow with the abstract. "Indonesia cut its quota 30%" before "states now set volume."
Every slide should have one signal sentence — a line that could be screenshot-quoted on its own. Short. Declarative. Often the headline.
Numbers carry the argument. Specific, sourced inline (J.P. Morgan, IEA, etc.), never rounded into vagueness. If a slide has no number, it has a named mechanism instead (a permit, a quota, a tariff, a law).
Use em dashes for pivots. Use italics sparingly — one conceptual word per slide, max — to mark the idea the sentence turns on.
Cut every transitional phrase ("Furthermore," "It's worth noting," "In today's world"). Slides are discontinuous; the reader supplies the connective tissue.
No adjectives where a number will do. No massive, huge, explosive, game-changing.
One pivot slide in the middle should name the thesis explicitly, often in the form "This isn't X. It's Y."
Vary slide weight: dense evidence slides earn a sparse one-line slide next to them. Rhythm matters.
Forbidden: rhetorical questions mid-deck, "imagine if…", "the future of X," exclamation marks, emoji, hype openers ("You won't believe…"), engagement-bait ("Agree? 👇").

For each slide, return:
- kicker
- headline
- body
- hero stat or quote (with source)
- speaker note (one sentence on what this slide is doing in the argument)

Last slide: the open question the argument leaves hanging. No CTA. No external link. The carousel must feel complete on its own.
