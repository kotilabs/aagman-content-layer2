## Core principles

### Brevity is power
Every word must earn its place. If you can say it in fewer words, do it.

### One idea per post
Each post contains one complete thought. The thread expands the argument, but each post must still stand alone if screenshot or quoted.

### Hook or die
The first post has one job: make someone stop scrolling. Lead with the shift, the tension, or the unusual fact — not the topic.

## Thread structure

- **Length:** 8–15 posts. Let the argument decide the length.
- **Post 1 — Hook:** Name the shift, not the topic. One sharp sentence. No rhetorical questions. No "imagine if."
- **Posts 2–N-1 — Body:** Each post advances the argument by one beat. Use numbers, named mechanisms, or sourced facts. One pivot post in the middle should name the thesis explicitly, often as "This isn't X. It's Y."
- **Post N — Open end:** End with unresolved cognitive tension — a variable worth watching, a structural tension, or a question that does not demand action. No CTA. No "follow for more." No engagement-bait.

## Voice rules

- Lead with the concrete, follow with the abstract. "Indonesia cut its quota 30%" before "states now set volume."
- Every post should have one signal sentence — a line that could be screenshot-quoted on its own. Short. Declarative.
- Numbers carry the argument. Specific, sourced inline (J.P. Morgan, IEA, RBI, etc.), never rounded into vagueness. If a post has no number, it has a named mechanism instead.
- Use em dashes for pivots. Use italics sparingly — one conceptual word per post, max.
- Cut transitional phrases ("Furthermore," "It's worth noting," "In today's world"). The reader supplies the connective tissue.
- No adjectives where a number will do. No massive, huge, explosive, game-changing.
- Vary post weight: dense evidence posts earn a sparse one-line post next to them. Rhythm matters.

## Hard constraints

- No stock tips, price targets, or return promises.
- No "AI will beat the market" narratives.
- No urgency bait ("act now", "don't miss").
- No moral superiority or trader shaming.
- No performative certainty.
- No rhetorical questions mid-thread.
- No "imagine if…" or "the future of X."
- No exclamation marks.
- No emoji.
- No hype openers ("You won't believe…").
- No engagement-bait ("Agree? 👇", "Reply with your take").

## Formatting

- Short sentences.
- Generous white space.
- Line breaks for emphasis.
- Number posts as 1/, 2/, 3/ etc.
- 0–1 hashtags max, or none.
- No links in the first post.

## Pre-publish check

Before finalizing, ask:
- Does each post stand alone if screenshotted?
- Does the thread respect uncertainty and separate facts from interpretations?
- Would this thread age well in 12–24 months?
- Does it end with a question or tension, not a conclusion or CTA?

If any answer is "no" → revise.
