## AI Data Centers Have Hit the Power-Semiconductor Wall

For two years, the AI story has been a compute story. NVIDIA's data-center revenue hit $193.7 billion. HBM sales are forecast at $54.6 billion. TSMC's CoWoS gap is narrowing.

The real bottleneck has moved:

• A single AI rack can draw 100+ kilowatts
• Power management IC lead times: 35–40 weeks
• Transformer lead times: 160+ weeks
• Mature-node 8-inch wafer capacity is shrinking in 2026

And the spending keeps running into the physical world:
• Hyperscaler AI capex: $600–700 billion this year
• US data-center electrical equipment market: $20B → $65B by 2030
• Copper demand from AI data centers: ~400,000 tonnes/year, peaking at 572,000 in 2028

Then the catch: you can order GPUs faster than you can energize the building that houses them.

That is what this week's blog, *AI Data Centers Have Hit the Power-Semiconductor Wall*, gets into. Why the scarce input is no longer the chip everyone talks about, what 1999–2000 and 2020–2022 teach us, and where the capital cycle goes when compute demand outruns the power to feed it.
