## China's Deflation Trap: Stimulus Without Demand

China closed 2025 with 5% GDP growth, exactly on target. Retail sales rose 3.7%. Industrial production grew 5.9%. The policy machinery looked like it was working.

The price data told a different story:

• Full-year CPI: 0.0%
• Producer prices: down 2.6%, the third consecutive yearly decline
• New-home prices in 70 cities: falling month-on-month
• Core CPI for the year: 0.7%

And the money is not going where prices are set:
• RMB 4.4 trillion in local-government special bonds
• RMB 1.3 trillion in ultra-long treasury bonds
• RMB 300 billion in consumer trade-ins — fully disbursed by September
• Urban households hold ~70% of wealth in property, which is still falling

Then the trap: Beijing is stimulating supply while households repair balance sheets. Growth and deflation can coexist.
