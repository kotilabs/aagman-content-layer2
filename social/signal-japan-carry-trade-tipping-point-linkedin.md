## Japan's Carry Trade Is Approaching a Structural Tipping Point

The Bank of Japan hiked to 1.0% in June, its highest rate since 1995. It is explicitly guiding for more. And yet the yen is near a 40-year low against the dollar.

The positioning underneath is what matters:

• CFTC leveraged funds: net short 90,461 yen futures contracts
• MOF spent ¥11.7 trillion buying yen in April–May
• The Fed–BoJ policy spread is still ~2.62%
• USD/JPY breached 163 in July

And the feedback loop is mechanical:
• A stronger yen forces yen-funded positions to close
• Closing means buying yen
• Buying yen means selling the assets bought with borrowed yen
• That selling pushes the yen higher

We saw the same channel in August 2024: a small BoJ hike helped trigger a 12.4% Nikkei drop and a VIX spike above 65. This time, the BoJ is not walking it back.
