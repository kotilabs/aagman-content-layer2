## Recommended concepts

### Concept 1: The Great Absorption
- **Central insight:** In the first five months of 2026, domestic institutions bought more than ₹4 lakh crore of Indian equities while foreign investors sold nearly ₹3.18 lakh crore — the largest cross-border ownership transfer in modern Indian market history.
- **Data to include:** DII monthly buying (Jan ₹69,220 cr; Feb ₹39,702 cr; Mar ~₹1.4 lakh cr; Apr ₹43,892 cr; May ₹82,669 cr; first five Jun sessions ₹33,933 cr) vs FII cumulative selling (~₹3.18 lakh cr Jan–May). Context: 2025 full-year FII outflows were ₹2.4 lakh crore; 2024 was ₹1.29 lakh crore.
- **Visualization type:** Diverging waterfall or mirrored bar chart. Left side: FII cumulative selling building downward; right side: DII cumulative buying building upward; center gap shows net domestic absorption.
- **Composition notes:** Reader sees the two opposing bars first — the visual tension between foreign exit and domestic entry. Monthly DII bars are stacked to show the accelerating May/March pulses. A small annotation at the bottom notes that five-month 2026 FII selling already exceeds full-year 2025. Payoff: India’s market is no longer hostage to a single marginal buyer.
- **Sources:** Kotak Neo, 9 Jun 2026; Moneycontrol, 5 May 2026.

### Concept 2: The Ownership Flip
- **Central insight:** For the first time in at least 14 years, domestic institutions own more of Indian equities than foreign portfolio investors.
- **Data to include:** FPI ownership 14.7% (14-year low) vs DII ownership 18.9% (record high) in April 2026; confirming Nifty 500 read: DII 20.9% vs FPI 17.1% as of March 2026; FII-to-DII ownership ratio 0.8x.
- **Visualization type:** Split-bar or side-by-side comparison with a subtle historical trace (small ghost bars for prior cycle highs/lows, e.g., FPI ownership peak vs current trough).
- **Composition notes:** Two horizontal bars dominate: the DII bar longer than the FPI bar for the first time. A thin ratio line below collapses from above 1.0x to 0.8x. Color coding makes domestic and foreign ownership instantly distinguishable. Payoff: the marginal price-setter has changed nationality.
- **Sources:** Wright Research / JM Financial, 9 Jun 2026; Motilal Oswal via Multibagg.ai, 7 May 2026.

### Concept 3: The Split Market
- **Central insight:** Foreign selling crushed the headline indices, but the domestic-flow-heavy mid- and small-cap segments barely moved — a two-speed market hidden inside one country.
- **Data to include:** BSE Sensex -13.7%; Nifty 50 -11.5%; BSE MidCap 150 -2.6%; BSE SmallCap 250 -0.5% (2026 through early June).
- **Visualization type:** Diverging slope chart or vertical lollipop chart. Four indices arranged left (large-cap) to right (small-cap), with red intensity fading toward neutral.
- **Composition notes:** Reader’s eye moves from the deeply negative Sensex/Nifty on the left to the nearly flat small-cap on the right. A short annotation explains *why*: FPI ownership is concentrated in large-cap financials/IT; domestic SIPs flow disproportionately into broader funds. Payoff: the index level is no longer the whole story.
- **Sources:** Kotak Neo, 9 Jun 2026.

### Concept 4: The Automatic Bid
- **Central insight:** While foreign investors sold, roughly 9.64 crore SIP accounts continued to deploy ₹30,954 crore every month, keeping equity mutual-fund inflows positive for the 63rd straight month.
- **Data to include:** May 2026 SIP inflow ₹30,954 crore; contributing accounts 9.64 crore; SIP assets ₹17.12 lakh crore (~21% of industry AUM); equity MF inflows ₹22,907.77 crore (lowest in 12 months but +63 consecutive months).
- **Visualization type:** Layered timeline. Top band: monthly SIP inflows above a ₹30,000 crore threshold line; bottom band: cumulative count of positive equity-MF months climbing to 63.
- **Composition notes:** A horizontal threshold line at ₹30,000 crore with monthly bars consistently clearing it. The 63-month streak is shown as a simple counter or progress bar. A small caveat: equity MF inflows fell 40% MoM in May, so the visual celebrates stickiness without overstating strength. Payoff: domestic flows are automatic, not emotional.
- **Sources:** AMFI via The Economic Times, 10 Jun 2026; Angel One, 10 Jun 2026.

### Concept 5: RBI’s Buffer vs. the Rupee
- **Central insight:** The rupee fell ~7% to a record low as foreign selling generated dollar demand, but the RBI held rates and used a $682 billion reserve buffer plus capital-account measures rather than hiking.
- **Data to include:** USD/INR ~₹89 at start of 2026 to ~₹96.34 record low on 19 May 2026 (~7% depreciation); RBI FX reserves $682.3 billion (29 May 2026); repo rate held at 5.25% (5 Jun 2026); capital-account measures: FAR expansion, FCNR(B) hedging support, NRI/OCI equity limits, concessional forex-swap window for PSU ECBs until 30 Sep 2026.
- **Visualization type:** Two-panel small-multiple. Left panel: rupee path with reserve-buffer shading underneath; right panel: policy-rate decision shown as a held-steady marker surrounded by the capital-account toolkit.
- **Composition notes:** Reader first sees the rupee slide, then notices the reserve cushion shaded below the path, then the policy panel showing "no hike." A connecting arrow makes clear the RBI chose capital-account tools over rates. Payoff: the external-sector shock is being managed, not ignored.
- **Sources:** Univest, 19 May 2026; The Hindu BusinessLine, 5 Jun 2026.

## What to avoid

- **FII outflow as a standalone scary number.** ₹3.18 lakh crore is dramatic, but without the DII absorption figure it tells the reader only that foreigners are selling — a story already everywhere in financial media.
- **Monthly ownership percentages without the comparison.** A single DII or FPI ownership percentage is not visually interesting until placed against the other and against history.
- **Rupee depreciation without the reserve/policy context.** A falling rupee chart invites panic; the interesting insight is the RBI’s response, so the rupee must be paired with reserves and the MPC decision.
- **Sector-level selling maps.** Financials accounted for ~51% of March FII selling, but the data is limited to one month and one source. A sector-concentration chart would be thin unless matched with a comparable DII sector-buying data set, which is available only as a broad claim (21 of 24 sectors) rather than precise monthly rupee flows.
- **FDI vs FPI full-year comparison.** While structurally interesting ($94.5 bn gross FDI vs -$16.67 bn net FPI), the FY26 fiscal year does not align cleanly with the Jan–May 2026 equity-flow window that drives the signal. It is better used as a footnote than a central visual.

## Final recommendation

Proceed with Concepts 1, 2, 3, and 4. Concept 5 is strong if paired with Concept 1 or 2, but works best as a follow-up rather than a standalone because the rupee story is a parallel market, not the equity ownership story itself.

Priority order for production:
1. Concept 1 — The Great Absorption (the clearest, most surprising single image).
2. Concept 2 — The Ownership Flip (the structural punchline).
3. Concept 3 — The Split Market (shows why the headline index is misleading).
4. Concept 4 — The Automatic Bid (explains the mechanism behind Concepts 1–3).
