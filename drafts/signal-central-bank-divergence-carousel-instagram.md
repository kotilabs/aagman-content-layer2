## Slide 1 — Hook

**Kicker:** June 2026

**Headline:** The three largest central banks just stopped coordinating.

**Body:** The Fed held but turned hawkish. The ECB hiked into a growth downgrade. The RBA paused after three straight increases. Even after the hike, the ECB's deposit rate at 2.25% remained the lowest of the three. For the first time in years, there is no single global policy anchor.

**Hero stat/quote:** Fed median 2026 dot: 3.8% (up from 3.4% in March) — *Federal Reserve, Summary of Economic Projections, 17 June 2026*

**Design note:** Split triptych — Fed, ECB, RBA logos on a dark field, each tagged HOLD / HIKE / PAUSE in institutional red, amber, and blue. Minimal typography; let the asymmetry do the work.

**Speaker note:** Names the divergence and establishes why the reader should stop scrolling.

## Slide 2 — The Fed

**Kicker:** The Fed

**Headline:** The Fed did not move — but half the committee now wants to hike.

**Body:** Rates stayed at 3.50%–3.75%. Yet nine of eighteen members project at least one increase this year, and the statement dropped its easing bias. One participant even projected a cut, underscoring the committee divide. Chair Warsh's first meeting was a communications reset dressed as a hold.

**Hero stat/quote:** 9 of 18 FOMC participants projected ≥1 rate hike by year-end 2026 — *Federal Reserve, Summary of Economic Projections, 17 June 2026*

**Design note:** Single bold number "9 of 18" centered on black, with the fed funds range as a thin horizontal band below. White and scarlet type only.

**Speaker note:** Shows that the hold was procedural, not dovish.

## Slide 3 — The ECB

**Kicker:** The ECB

**Headline:** The ECB hiked for the first time since 2023 — into a downgrade.

**Body:** All three key rates rose 25bp. Lagarde rejected the label "insurance hike." But the bank also cut its 2026 euro-area growth forecast to 0.8%, and headline inflation is projected to stay above 2% through 2027.

**Hero stat/quote:** ECB deposit facility rate: 2.25% (+25bp); 2026 GDP projection: 0.8% — *European Central Bank, Monetary policy decisions, 11 June 2026*

**Design note:** A two-step staircase: rate up on the left, growth forecast down on the right. Small euro symbol anchored in the lower corner. Muted ECB blue-grey palette.

**Speaker note:** Captures the ECB's uncomfortable credibility trade-off.

## Slide 4 — The RBA

**Kicker:** The RBA

**Headline:** Australia hiked three times, then stopped to watch.

**Body:** The RBA left the cash rate at 4.35%. Inflation is "still too high," but unemployment has risen to 4.5% and Q1 GDP slowed to 0.3% q/q. The phrase "further if required" stayed in the statement — the pause is conditional, not conclusive.

**Hero stat/quote:** RBA cash rate target: 4.35% (hold), after 25bp hikes in February, March, and May — *Reserve Bank of Australia, 16 June 2026*

**Design note:** A single horizontal line at 4.35% with three small ascending ticks behind it for the prior hikes. Sydney sandstone and reserve-bank green.

**Speaker note:** Introduces the third regime — a domestic-demand pause.

## Slide 5 — The oil unwind

**Kicker:** The oil unwind

**Headline:** Brent crude nearly halved in two months while central banks were still tightening.

**Body:** From roughly $140 in April to below $73 by late June. The interim U.S.–Iran memorandum reopened Hormuz shipping faster than the policy cycle could adjust.

**Hero stat/quote:** Brent crude: ~$72.95/bbl on 26 June, down ~21% over the month — *Trading Economics, 27 June 2026*

**Design note:** A steep descending price line from April to June, with a small tanker icon at the June endpoint. OPEC beige and diplomatic blue; no chart junk.

**Speaker note:** Adds the supply-shock reversal that complicates every central bank's story.

## Slide 6 — Market read

**Kicker:** Market read

**Headline:** By late June: the dollar broke 100; the curve flattened; gold sold off.

**Body:** Currencies price the expected path, not the current decision. The front end repriced higher; the long end caught falling oil. Higher real yields made non-yielding assets expensive. Front end up, long end down — a policy repricing, not a growth scare.

**Hero stat/quote:** 2-year Treasury yield rose ~10bp; 30-year yield fell ~7bp; DXY broke above 100 — *Mariemont Capital / Kenmacro, week ending 19 June 2026*

**Design note:** Three vertical panels: DXY crossing 100, a twisting yield curve, a falling gold bar. One accent colour per panel — dollar green, Treasury grey, gold amber — on a dark ground.

**Speaker note:** Translates policy divergence into cross-asset prices.

## Slide 7 — Thesis

**Kicker:** Thesis

**Headline:** This is not a global easing cycle. Markets are treating it as three different inflation regimes.

**Body:** The U.S. faces a demand-pulse repricing. Europe faces an energy-import shock. Australia faces domestic capacity pressure. One size never fit all; now markets have to price the seams.

**Hero stat/quote:** "17 of 18 FOMC participants judged risks to inflation as weighted to the upside" — *Mariemont Capital, 22 June 2026*

**Design note:** Three diverging lines on a single sparse chart, each labelled US / Euro area / Australia. Clean white space; this is the pivot slide — let it breathe.

**Speaker note:** States the central argument explicitly.
