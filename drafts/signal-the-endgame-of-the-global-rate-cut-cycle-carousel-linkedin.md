## Slide 1

- **Kicker:** The shift
- **Headline:** The central bank cutting cycle is over — for now.
- **Body:** The Fed, ECB and BoJ are no longer easing. The question is no longer how fast they will cut, but what happens when rates stop well above the 2010s floor. This is the base-case interpretation, not a certainty.
- **Hero stat:** Fed funds target range 3.50–3.75%, unchanged since December 2025; confirmed June 2026 FOMC. *Source: FXStreet / Advisor Perspectives.*
- **Design note:** White background, oversized headline, small-caps central bank initials in the corner.
- **Speaker note:** Names the regime shift and introduces the core tension.

## Slide 2

- **Kicker:** Dot plot
- **Headline:** The Fed now sees 3.8% at year-end and a longer-run rate of 3.1%.
- **Body:** Between March and June 2026 the 2026 projection rose 40 basis points, from 3.4% to 3.8%. The path is being revised up, not down.
- **Hero stat:** +40 bps revision to the 2026 fed funds projection; longer-run neutral estimate 3.1%. *Source: Advisor Perspectives / intellectia.ai.*
- **Design note:** A simple stepped line showing the March-to-June upward revision.
- **Speaker note:** Grounds the regime shift in FOMC projections.

## Slide 3

- **Kicker:** The constraint
- **Headline:** US inflation is above target on headline, core, and the Fed's own projection.
- **Body:** Headline CPI is 4.2%, core CPI is 2.9%, and the 2026 PCE inflation projection is 3.6%. The Fed is unlikely to ease while projecting an inflation overshoot.
- **Hero stat:** 2026 PCE inflation projection 3.6% YoY; US CPI 4.2% YoY; core CPI 2.9% YoY. *Source: intellectia.ai / Cambridge Currencies.*
- **Design note:** Three numbers in a row, each with a clean label beneath.
- **Speaker note:** Shows why the inflation mandate limits the Fed.

## Slide 4

- **Kicker:** Beyond the Fed
- **Headline:** The ECB hiked from 2.0% for the first time since September 2023; the BoJ reached 1.00%.
- **Body:** The ECB deposit facility rate was 2.0% in June 2025; the new level is not specified in the research. The Bank of Japan raised its policy rate to 1.00% on 16 June 2026. The global easing narrative is on hold — for now.
- **Hero stat:** ECB deposit facility rate raised from 2.0% in June 2026; BoJ policy rate 1.00% on 16 June 2026. *Source: Statista / Finance Calendar.*
- **Design note:** World map with two callouts — Frankfurt and Tokyo.
- **Speaker note:** Broadens the argument beyond the US and notes data limitations.

## Slide 5

- **Kicker:** The stock
- **Headline:** Gross global debt is heading for 100% of GDP by 2029.
- **Body:** Government interest payments are near 3% of global GDP. The aggregate masks very different dynamics in the US, euro area and Japan, but the carry cost is rising everywhere.
- **Hero stat:** Gross global debt 100% of GDP by 2029; global government interest payments ~3% of global GDP. *Source: CRFB / IMF Fiscal Monitor, May 2026.*
- **Design note:** A single bar climbing toward a 100% marker.
- **Speaker note:** Introduces the debt-sustainability channel without implying a uniform crisis.

## Slide 6

- **Kicker:** The Aagman thesis
- **Headline:** This isn't a pause in the cutting cycle. It's the *endgame* of easy money.
- **Body:** Our interpretation: rates are too low to force rapid deleveraging, but too high to allow the aggressive stimulus the 2010s taught markets to expect.
- **Hero stat:** "Rates are too low to force rapid deleveraging, but too high to allow the kind of aggressive stimulus that markets became accustomed to in the 2010s." — Aagman / research artifact.
- **Design note:** Split layout with "Pause" struck through and "Endgame" in bold.
- **Speaker note:** Names the thesis explicitly and frames it as an interpretation.

## Slide 7

- **Kicker:** Valuation
- **Headline:** A sustained 3.5–4.0% policy rate anchors a higher risk-free hurdle.
- **Body:** The policy rate anchors the hurdle; the Treasury curve is the practical discount rate. A cash flow ten years out is worth less today at 4% than at 1%.
- **Hero stat:** Fed funds target range 3.50–3.75%; longer-run estimate 3.1%. *Source: FOMC / Advisor Perspectives.*
- **Design note:** Two overlapping discount curves — one low, one higher — with present-value bars shrinking on the right.
- **Speaker note:** Explains the discount-rate channel without conflating fed funds with the risk-free curve.

## Slide 8

- **Kicker:** The counter
- **Headline:** In real terms, policy may still be accommodative.
- **Body:** With headline CPI at 4.2%, the ex-post real Fed funds rate is roughly -0.5% to -0.7%. That is the strongest argument against calling the current regime meaningfully tight.
- **Hero stat:** Ex-post real Fed funds rate ~-0.5% to -0.7% vs May 2026 headline CPI. *Source: Aagman calculation from Cambridge Currencies / FOMC.*
- **Design note:** A small chart showing the nominal rate sitting below the inflation line.
- **Speaker note:** Surfaces the strongest competing interpretation to keep the argument honest.

## Slide 9

- **Kicker:** Allocation
- **Headline:** Many long-duration assets were priced for a lower-rate world.
- **Body:** Long-dated cash flows, leveraged structures and parts of commercial real estate face refinancing pressure. Cash-flow-positive, shorter-duration equities face less.
- **Hero stat:** IMF global growth 3.0% in 2026; global headline inflation 4.7% in 2026. *Source: IMF July 2026 WEO / Xinhua / Anadolu / IDN Financials.*
- **Design note:** A duration bar: long-duration assets on the left under pressure, shorter-duration assets on the right.
- **Speaker note:** Translates the rate regime into capital-allocation consequences without stock tips.
