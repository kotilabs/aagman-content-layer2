## Recommended concepts

### Concept 1: The Great Absorption — DII buying vs. FPI selling
- **Central insight:** Domestic institutions bought ₹4.16 lakh crore of Indian equities in 2026 while foreign investors sold ₹2.7 lakh crore — one of the largest ownership transfers in recent Indian market history.
- **Data:** DII net purchase ₹4.16 lakh crore; FPI net sell ₹2.7 lakh crore (Jan – early June 2026). Monthly DII buying peaked at ₹1,36,000 crore in March 2026. Source: NiftyTrader.
- **Visualization type:** Diverging bar chart or two-sided waterfall.
- **Composition notes:** Left side shows FPI selling by month; right side shows DII buying by month. The eye is drawn to the March 2026 spike where both forces collided. A narrow connecting band emphasizes that domestic buying roughly offset foreign selling. Payoff: the market’s marginal buyer has changed hands.
- **Sources:** NiftyTrader, "DII Buying Hits ₹4.16 Lakh Crore in 2026" (https://www.niftytrader.in/markets/dii-buying-hits-rs-4-16-lakh-crore-in-2026/)

### Concept 2: The SIP Engine — how mechanical inflows became a market backstop
- **Central insight:** Monthly SIP contributions held above ₹31,000 crore for five straight months, creating a recurring bid that is largely price-insensitive.
- **Data:** SIP contributions: April ₹31,115 crore; May ₹30,954 crore; June ₹31,781 crore; March peak ₹32,087 crore. SIP AUM ₹17,70,482 crore; 1,051.59 lakh outstanding accounts. Source: AMFI / RightAdvise.
- **Visualization type:** Stacked area chart or flowing pipeline diagram.
- **Composition notes:** Top half shows the monthly SIP stream flowing into equity mutual funds; bottom half shows the resulting DII equity purchases. The width of the flow is proportional to the rupee amount. Payoff: a mechanical inflow is doing the work that discretionary foreign capital used to do.
- **Sources:** Outlook Money, "Amfi Data June 2026" (https://www.outlookmoney.com/invest/amfi-data-june-2026-equity-mutual-fund-inflows-aum-etf-sip); RightAdvise, "SIP Data India" (https://rightadvise.com/sip-data-india.html)

### Concept 3: The calm surface, the moving fault line — VIX, Nifty, and the rupee
- **Central insight:** Record foreign selling did not crash the index or spike volatility; the pressure escaped through the currency instead.
- **Data:** FPI cumulative selling since Sep 2024 peak: ~$64 billion; FPI equity AUC fell from $931B to $726B (-22%). Nifty 50: high 26,340 (2 Jan 2026), close 24,211 (13 Jul 2026). India VIX: 9.18 (2 Jan) to 13.28 (13 Jul). USD/INR: 95.65 (13 Jul 2026). Sources: Finnovate; The Hindu BusinessLine; ScanX Trade; Angel One.
- **Visualization type:** Three-panel line chart with shared x-axis (Jan – Jul 2026).
- **Composition notes:** Panel 1 shows FPI cumulative selling; Panel 2 shows Nifty 50 and India VIX; Panel 3 shows USD/INR. The reader sees selling accelerate while the index flatlines and the rupee weakens. Payoff: the market is range-bound not because risk disappeared, but because the pressure valve moved to FX.
- **Sources:** Finnovate, "FPI Flows June 2026" (https://www.finnovate.in/learn/blog/fpi-flows-june-2026-india-sectoral-analysis); The Hindu BusinessLine (https://www.thehindubusinessline.com/markets/sensex-nifty50-today-stock-market-live-updates-13-july-2026/article71213684.ece); ScanX Trade (https://scanx.trade/stock-market-news/stocks/indian-markets-begin-2026-with-range-bound-trading-amid-continued-fii-selling/28861466); Angel One (https://www.angelone.in/indices/india-vix)

### Concept 4: Rotation, not exit — FPI equity selling vs. debt buying
- **Central insight:** In June 2026, foreign investors sold $5.21 billion of Indian equities but bought enough Indian government bonds that debt inflows exceeded equity outflows by $500 million.
- **Data:** FPI June equity selling: $5.21 billion. FPI debt inflows vs. equity outflows: +$500 million net (debt > equity). Source: Finnovate / NSDL.
- **Visualization type:** Sankey or split-flow diagram.
- **Composition notes:** One stream enters India; it splits into equity (red, flowing out) and debt (green, flowing in). The net is not an exit but a rotation into FAR bonds. Payoff: the FPI story is more about asset-class preference than a wholesale India retreat.
- **Sources:** Finnovate, "FPI Flows June 2026: Selling Reaccelerates to $5.21 Billion" (https://www.finnovate.in/learn/blog/fpi-flows-june-2026-india-sectoral-analysis)

## What to avoid

- **Avoid a simple "DIIs saved the market" hero narrative.** The data shows absorption, not bullish conviction. The mechanical nature of SIP flows is the interesting tension.
- **Avoid equating low VIX with low risk.** VIX compression in the presence of record FPI selling is the anomaly; presenting it as stability misses the FX pressure-release mechanism.
- **Avoid sectoral breakdowns unless verified.** The research notes FPI selling concentrated in oil & gas, autos, metals, IT, and financials, but lacks precise sectoral rupee figures suitable for a standalone visual.
- **Avoid forward-looking claims about FPI re-entry or SIP growth.** The data is descriptive; projections would be interpretation.

## Final recommendation

Proceed with Concepts 1, 2, 3, and 4. Concept 1 is the strongest single visual because it captures the ownership-transfer thesis at a glance. Concept 3 provides the essential cross-asset context (equities calm, rupee weak). Concepts 2 and 4 deepen the mechanism without overstating the case.
