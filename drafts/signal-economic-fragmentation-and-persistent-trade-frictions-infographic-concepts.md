## Recommended concepts

### Concept 1: The Great Rerouting — U.S. import share shift
- **Central insight:** China's share of U.S. imports has collapsed back toward pre-WTO levels, while Vietnam, India, and Mexico absorbed the redirect.
- **Data:** China share 13.4% (2024) → 9.3% (Jan–Oct 2025); Vietnam imports +42.0% to $193.8 billion; India imports $103.8 billion; Mexico total trade $872.8 billion.
- **Visualization type:** Diverging bar or Sankey-style flow diagram.
- **Composition notes:** Left side shows China's shrinking share as a thinning arrow. Right side shows widening arrows to Vietnam, India, Mexico. A small annotation notes that many of these flows still embed Chinese components.
- **Sources:** White House ERP 2026, USTR, U.S. Census Bureau, CRS.

### Concept 2: The Tariff Stack — why headline rates mislead
- **Central insight:** The effective barrier on Chinese goods is a stack of duties and controls, not a single rate.
- **Data:** Section 301 (up to 25–50% on some categories), Section 232 sectoral duties, 10% Section 122 global surcharge, fentanyl-related IEEPA duties, BIS case-by-case semiconductor licensing effective January 15, 2026.
- **Visualization type:** Stacked layered diagram or iceberg graphic.
- **Composition notes:** Top of the stack shows the visible 10% headline. Below the waterline are the Section 301, 232, IEEPA, and licensing layers. A note: "The exact rate depends on HS code, origin, and which layers apply."
- **Sources:** White House ERP 2026, Prodensa, Morgan Lewis, CRS.

### Concept 3: Fragmentation vs. Collapse — trade volume vs. composition
- **Central insight:** Global trade is growing in value and still positive in volume, but its composition and routing are politically fragmenting.
- **Data:** World merchandise trade +4.6% in 2025, +1.9% forecast 2026; global trade value record $35 trillion; WTO estimates 72% of world trade still under MFN rules.
- **Visualization type:** Dual-axis line chart or split-panel comparison.
- **Composition notes:** Top panel: rising nominal trade value. Bottom panel: slowing real volume growth. A thin band notes strategic sectors moving outside MFN rules.
- **Sources:** WTO, UNCTAD.

### Concept 4: India's China deficit — the decoupling paradox
- **Central insight:** India is gaining U.S. market share while its deficit with China widens, showing rerouting is not the same as independence.
- **Data:** India-China deficit $99.2 billion FY24–25, projected ~$111.4 billion FY25–26; U.S. imports from India $103.8 billion in 2025; U.S. trade deficit with India $58.4 billion.
- **Visualization type:** Two-sided balance-scale or diverging bar chart.
- **Composition notes:** One side shows India's rising exports to the U.S. and bilateral surplus. The other side shows India's rising imports from China and widening deficit. The visual asks which force is larger.
- **Sources:** U.S. Census Bureau, The Hindu, BasisPoint Insight (GTRI).

## What to avoid

- **Simple "deglobalisation" collapse narrative.** Trade volumes are still positive and nominal value is at a record. A graphic that implies trade is shrinking would mislead.
- **Country-ranking scorecard without mechanism.** Showing Vietnam as "winner" without noting the China-plus-one rerouting risk would oversimplify.
- **Semiconductor chip diagram without licensing context.** The BIS change is from presumptive denial to case-by-case review, not deregulation. Visuals must avoid implying controls were lifted.
- **Price target or market-call imagery.** No arrows pointing up on stock indices, no "buy" or "sell" framing, no return promises.

## Final recommendation

Proceed with Concepts 1, 2, and 3 as the strongest visual case. Concept 4 is strong but works best as a supporting panel paired with Concept 1. Concept 2 is the most distinctive because it surfaces a hidden mechanism—the effective tariff stack—that headline rate charts miss.
