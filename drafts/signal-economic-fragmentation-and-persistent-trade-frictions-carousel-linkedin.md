# Carousel: Economic Fragmentation and Persistent Trade Frictions

## Slide 1
- **Kicker:** The signal
- **Headline:** Trade barriers were recast, not removed.
- **Body:** The U.S. and China struck truces in 2025. Tariff headlines fell. The effective barrier stack did not.
- **Hero stat or quote:** Section 301 tariffs on China remain; Section 122 10% global surcharge still collected as of July 2026.
- **Design note:** Split background: calm diplomatic imagery on one side, layered tariff document stack on the other.
- **Speaker note:** Establish the central tension: pause, not peace.

- **Headline:** U.S. imports from China dropped by $97.1 billion in ten months.
- **Body:** January–October 2025 vs the same period in 2024. China's share of U.S. imports fell to 9.3% from 13.4%.

## Slide 4
- **Kicker:** The pivot
- **Headline:** This isn't reshoring. It's rerouting.
- **Body:** Final assembly moves to a third country. Chinese components, capital equipment, and intermediate goods keep flowing into those hubs.
- **Hero stat or quote:** India's trade deficit with China: $99.2 billion in FY24–25, projected ~$111.4 billion in FY25–26.

## Slide 5

- **Headline:** Effective tariffs are now a stack.
- **Body:** Section 301 + Section 232 + Section 122 + fentanyl-related duties. The rate depends on HS code, origin, and which layers apply.
- **Hero stat or quote:** 10% — Section 122 global surcharge, due to expire July 24, 2026.

- **Speaker note:** Explain why headline rates understate the barrier.

## Slide 6
- **Kicker:** The nuance
- **Headline:** Trade is still growing.

- **Design note:** Diverging lines: trade value rising, trade growth rate slowing.
- **Speaker note:** Defeat the simplistic collapse narrative.
