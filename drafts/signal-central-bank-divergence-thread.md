1/
The coordinated global easing story died in mid-June. The Fed, ECB and RBA delivered a split verdict that leaves the dollar bloc without a single policy anchor.

2/
The Fed held at 3.50%–3.75% on June 17. Then it raised the median 2026 dot to 3.8% from 3.4%. Nine of eighteen FOMC members now project at least one hike this year; one participant projected a cut.

3/
The Fed also revised 2026 PCE inflation to 3.6% from 2.7%, and core PCE to 3.3% from 2.7%. Seventeen of eighteen participants judged inflation risks as tilted to the upside.

4/
On June 11 the ECB hiked all three key rates by 25bp — its first increase since September 2023 — into a growth outlook cut to 0.8% for 2026. Lagarde called it a monetary-policy decision, not an insurance hike.

5/
The RBA left the cash rate at 4.35% on June 16, after hikes in February, March and May. It kept the conditional hiking bias: inflation is still too high and rates can rise further if required.

6/
Brent crude fell to roughly $73 by June 26, down about 21% over the month and nearly 50% from its April peak near $140. The U.S.–Iran memorandum signed on June 18 reopened the Hormuz Strait narrative.

7/
This is not a synchronized tightening cycle. It is three central banks reading the same oil shock through three different economies — and arriving at three different terminal rates.

8/
Currencies price the expected path, not the current rate. By late June, EUR/USD had fallen below 1.15 and the DXY had broken 100 — even after the ECB actually hiked.

9/
The Treasury curve twist-flattened. The 2-year yield rose roughly 10bp while the 30-year yield fell roughly 7bp. The front end repriced Fed hawkishness; the long end priced oil relief.

10/
Gold sold off toward $4,155–$4,220 as markets priced higher real U.S. yields, raising the opportunity cost of holding non-yielding bullion. U.S. equities fell on the hawkish surprise.

11/
The open question is whether the Fed will follow its dot plot, or whether the dot plot will follow oil. If Brent holds near $70–$75, the case for a 2026 hike may look stale by autumn.
