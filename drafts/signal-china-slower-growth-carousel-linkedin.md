# LinkedIn Standalone Carousel — China Slower Growth

**Signal ID:** china-slower-growth  
**Platform:** LinkedIn (standalone, no external link)  
**Slide count:** 10  
**Voice:** FT leader column × Matt Levine — dry, confident, structurally argued

---

## Slide 1

**Kicker:** The split

**Headline:** China's economy is slowing on the inside and strong on the outside.

**Body:** May retail sales contracted 0.6% year-on-year while exports surged to a record $376.78 billion. The headline GDP number hides two economies moving in opposite directions.

**Hero stat or quote:** "May retail sales fell 0.6% y/y — the first contraction in the current cycle." — National Bureau of Statistics of China, *Total Retail Sales of Consumer Goods in May 2026*, 17 June 2026

**Design note:** Split-screen visual — an empty shopping district on the left, a busy container port on the right; headline spans the divide in a single line.

**Speaker note:** Open with the contradiction that frames the entire argument.

---

## Slide 2

**Kicker:** Domestic demand

**Headline:** Chinese consumers are pulling back.

**Body:** Retail sales were already weak in April at +0.2%, the softest since late 2022. In May they turned negative, with month-on-month growth at -0.38%. Households are saving more, borrowing less, and repaying debt faster than they spend.

**Hero stat or quote:** "Household debt contracted by CNY 786.9 billion in April 2026, the largest monthly drop on record." — China Banking News, *How China plans to avoid a Japanese-style balance sheet recession*, 21 May 2026

**Design note:** A downward-sloping retail-sales line chart; single red data point at May 2026.

**Speaker note:** Establish the domestic-demand problem with the most concrete evidence.

---

## Slide 3

**Kicker:** Property

**Headline:** Real estate is still the largest contractionary force in the economy.

**Body:** Property investment fell 16.2% in the first five months of 2026, accelerating from a 13.7% decline through April. New home prices across 70 cities have now fallen for 35 consecutive months. First-tier cities show tentative stabilisation, but lower-tier cities are still falling and the national drag has not reversed.

**Hero stat or quote:** "New home prices across 70 cities fell 3.5% y/y in May 2026 — the 35th consecutive annual decline." — NBS via Trading Economics

**Design note:** Photograph of unfinished residential towers; overlay with "35 months" in large, sober type.

**Speaker note:** Show that the property drag is deepening, not stabilising.

---

## Slide 4

**Kicker:** Investment

**Headline:** The weakness has spread beyond real estate.

**Body:** Fixed-asset investment contracted 4.1% year-on-year through May, worse than the 1.6% decline through April. Even excluding property, investment has turned negative — a sign the slowdown is broadening across the domestic economy.

**Hero stat or quote:** "Fixed-asset investment contracted 4.1% y/y in Jan–May 2026, with May alone down 1.91% m/m." — NBS via Trading Economics

**Design note:** Bar chart comparing Jan–Apr and Jan–May FAI growth; both bars below the zero line.

**Speaker note:** Broaden the argument from property to the whole investment cycle.

---

## Slide 5

**Kicker:** The offset

**Headline:** Exports and industrial production are masking the damage.

**Body:** Industrial production rebounded to 4.5% in May, and exports jumped 19.4% to a record $376.78 billion. AI-related semiconductors and front-loading ahead of tariff uncertainty are driving the outperformance.

**Hero stat or quote:** "China's exports surged 19.4% y/y in May 2026 to a record USD 376.78 billion." — General Administration of Customs via Trading Economics

**Design note:** World map with export routes highlighted; the record monthly export number dominant in the upper-right corner.

**Speaker note:** Introduce the production/export track that keeps headline GDP from collapsing.

---

## Slide 6

**Kicker:** Thesis

**Headline:** This isn't a collapse. It's a *two-speed* economy.

**Body:** One track — consumption, property, investment — is shrinking. The other — exports, manufacturing, technology — is expanding. Because domestic demand carries the larger weight in GDP, the net result is slower growth, not a crash.

**Hero stat or quote:** "Q1 2026 real GDP grew 5.0% y/y; Q2 is widely expected to fall meaningfully short." — Piraeus Group, *Global Macro Trends — June 2026*

**Design note:** Two diverging lines on one chart; use a single italicised "two-speed" in the headline. Keep the rest of the slide clean.

**Speaker note:** Name the thesis explicitly halfway through the deck.

---

## Slide 7

**Kicker:** Credit

**Headline:** Monetary easing is not reviving credit demand.

**Body:** The PBOC has pledged RRR and rate cuts, but outstanding RMB loan growth is at a roughly two-decade low and total social financing growth has slowed to the high single digits. Banks have liquidity; households and firms are repaying rather than borrowing. May TSF flow rebounded sharply to CNY 2.03 trillion, but stock growth and loan demand remain weak. The surveyed urban unemployment rate ticked down to 5.1%, so the weakness is showing up in balance sheets and prices before jobs. CPI is below target while PPI rises on input costs — a sign of weak final demand, not overheating.

**Hero stat or quote:** "Outstanding RMB loans to the real economy grew 5.6% y/y in April 2026, the slowest in roughly two decades." — PBOC via CGTN / DBS Bank

**Design note:** Funnel graphic showing liquidity injection thinning before it reaches borrowers; the bottom of the funnel labelled "credit demand."

**Speaker note:** Explain why policy levers are not yet reversing the domestic trend.

---

## Slide 8

**Kicker:** Spillovers

**Headline:** The slowdown is already leaving China through lower import demand.

**Body:** Iron ore has fallen 8.18% over the past month. China's record trade surplus reflects weak imports as much as strong exports. Asian supply chains and commodity currencies are the first transmission points. Bond yields are pricing weak growth; the yuan is stable on the record surplus.

**Hero stat or quote:** "Iron ore (62% Fe, CFR Tianjin) fell to USD 100.33/metric ton on 26 June 2026, down 8.18% over the past month." — Trading Economics

**Design note:** Commodity price ticker falling; map of Asian trade routes dimming from China outward.

**Speaker note:** Show the global read-through of China's domestic shortfall.

---

## Slide 9

**Kicker:** Beijing

**Headline:** Beijing has already lowered its growth ambition.

**Body:** The 2026 GDP target was cut to 4.5%–5.0%, and the headline fiscal deficit is held near 4% of GDP. The response so far is targeted easing and expanded trade-in programmes, not the larger demand-side stimulus that would be required to flip the domestic track quickly.

**Hero stat or quote:** "China lowered its 2026 GDP growth target to 4.5%–5.0%, down from roughly 5% for 2025." — MarketWatch / New Straits Times, 5 March 2026

**Design note:** Official document with the target range highlighted; the 10-year CGB yield line falling in the background as a market verdict.

**Speaker note:** Set up the final question about whether Beijing will tolerate or reverse the slowdown.

---

## Slide 10

**Kicker:** Open question

**Headline:** Will Beijing tolerate the split, or spend big enough to close it?

**Body:** If exports and production remain strong, China can hit its lower target without a major stimulus. If the export boom is front-loading that unwinds in the second half, the domestic track will dominate — and the policy choice will become unavoidable.

**Hero stat or quote:** "USD/CNY was 6.80 on 26 June 2026, stable despite weak domestic data." — Trading Economics, "Chinese Yuan"

**Design note:** Two diverging paths on a road; no CTA, no external link, no hashtag.

**Speaker note:** End with the unresolved tension that determines the next chapter of the story.
