1/ The rupee is at 95. The Nifty is flat. Both cannot be right about the same economy.

2/ FPIs sold ~USD 27.7 billion of Indian equities in the four months March–June 2026. The most concentrated four-month outflow on record.

3/ Cumulative FPI selling since the September 2024 peak: ~USD 64 billion. By mid-June, 2026 outflows hit ₹2.87 lakh crore — more than all of 2025.

4/ The rupee reflects that pressure. USD/INR reference rate: 95.4346 on 6 July. Trade deficit widened to USD 30.43 billion in June from USD 19.12 billion a year ago.

5/ Yet the Nifty closed at 24,207 on 13 July. Weekly range: 23,805–24,530. India VIX: 12.25. No panic.

6/ This is not foreign absence. It is domestic absorption.

7/ DIIs bought ~₹4.16 lakh crore of equities from January to early June. FPIs sold ~₹2.7 lakh crore. On 1 July alone, DII buying was nearly 3x FPI selling.

8/ SIP flows hit ₹31,781 crore in June. Five straight months above ₹30,000 crore. The bid is recurring and relatively price-insensitive.

9/ FPIs are not fleeing India entirely. In June, debt inflows exceeded equity outflows by USD 500 million. Rotation, not flight.

10/ This isn't a balance-of-payments crisis. It is a market-structure transition.

11/ Four readings: domestic absorption has matured / FX is pricing risk equities ignore / different markets, different horizons / passive rebalancing and sector rotation.

12/ The 2013 Taper Tantrum comparison fails on the current account. Then: ~4.7% of GDP. Now: ~0.2–1.0%. Reserves then: thin. Now: above USD 700 billion.

13/ The 2022 comparison is closer — geopolitical oil shock, FPI exit, domestic flows absorbing. But the rupee has now moved well past the 2022 low.

14/ The RBI is on hold at 5.25% with a neutral stance. CPI is back above 4% after 16–17 months. Oil and the monsoon are the swing variables.

15/ If the domestic bid is price-insensitive, it can hold forever — until it doesn't. The question is whether the rupee is warning about a risk equities will eventually have to price.
