## Signal summary

The Fed, ECB and RBA have delivered the most divergent major-central-bank configuration in years: the Fed held with hawkish dots, the ECB hiked into a growth downgrade, and the RBA paused after three 2026 hikes. Against that backdrop, the Reserve Bank of India held the repo rate at 5.25% and opened a suite of dollar-inflow measures — FAR expansion, FCNR(B) support, and easier NRI/OCI equity limits — to improve external buffers without tightening into a domestic slowdown. The combined story is how a large emerging-market central bank preserves optionality while the developed-world policy anchor fragments.
