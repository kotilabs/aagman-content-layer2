## Signal summary

Brent crude oil and gold have sold off sharply in late June 2026 as the US–Iran war risk premium has been largely removed, even though the 18 June agreement is only a 14-point memorandum of understanding that initiates a 60-day negotiating window. By late June Brent had fallen to roughly $72–$73/bbl and gold to approximately $4,000–$4,100/oz. The setup is asymmetric: if the 60-day talks stall, demining is delayed, or regional actors object, the risk premium could rebuild quickly.
