## Signal summary

April–May 2026 activity data confirm that China’s economy is slowing and splitting into two tracks: domestic demand is weakening (retail sales contracted in May, fixed-asset investment is negative, property investment is down double digits) while production and exports remain strong. The signal matters because a sustained domestic-demand shortfall would lower global import demand, weigh on commodities and Asian manufacturing, and test Beijing’s policy response.
