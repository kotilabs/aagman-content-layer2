## Recommended concepts

### Concept 1: The June divergence — four central banks, four signals
- **Central insight:** For the first time in years the major developed central banks are no longer moving in lockstep, and the RBI chose to keep its policy rate on hold while they sorted out their divergent paths.
- **Data to include:**
  - Fed: held at **3.50%–3.75%**, median 2026 dot **3.8%** (up from 3.4% in March), **9/18** participants projecting ≥1 hike.
  - ECB: raised deposit facility to **2.25%** (+25bp), first hike since Sep 2023, 2026 GDP growth cut to **0.8%**.
  - RBA: held at **4.35%** after 25bp hikes in Feb, Mar, May 2026.
  - RBI: held repo at **5.25%**, neutral stance, FY27 GDP cut to **6.6%**, CPI raised to **5.1%**.
- **Visualization type:** Four-column comparison matrix / small-multiple panels.
- **Rough composition notes:** A single headline across the top: "No shared global anchor." Each bank gets a vertical panel with three rows — current rate, latest move, forward bias — using colour (red = tighter/hawkish, amber = on hold/conditional, green = unchanged/supportive). The reader scans left-to-right from Fed to RBI; the payoff is that India’s neutral stance is rational precisely because the others disagree.
- **Sources:** Federal Reserve, 17 Jun 2026; ECB, 11 Jun 2026; RBA, 16 Jun 2026; RBI, 5 Jun 2026.

### Concept 2: India’s capital-account toolbox
- **Central insight:** The RBI kept the repo rate at **5.25%** and used five capital-account levers — plus a G-Sec tax exemption — to attract dollars without choking domestic growth.
- **Data to include:**
  - Five RBI measures: FAR expansion to 15-/30-/40-year G-Secs; higher NRI/OCI/PROI equity limits; concessional forex swaps for PSU ECBs until 30 Sep 2026; FCNR(B) hedge-cost absorption until 30 Sep 2026 (RBI absorbs **~280–300 bps**); export-proceeds realisation restored to nine months.
  - Government ordinance: FII/BIS tax exemption on G-Sec interest and capital gains, effective retrospectively from 1 Apr 2026.
  - Pressure to offset: net FPI outflows **$13.7 billion** FY27 so far (till 2 Jun).
  - Buffer: forex reserves **$682.3 billion**.
  - Early response: FPI bond inflows **₹35,000 crore** as of 23 Jun 2026.
- **Visualization type:** Flow chart / toolbox diagram.
- **Rough composition notes:** An open toolbox sits in the centre. Each lever is a labelled tool feeding into a "dollar inflow" bucket. To the left, a red outflow pipe shows **$13.7 bn** in net FPI outflows. To the right, a reserve tank shows **$682.3 bn**. A small green arrow labelled **₹35,000 cr** bond inflows points back into the bucket as the first measured result. Use a distinct colour or annotation for the government ordinance so it is clearly shown as a separate, complementary measure rather than an RBI tool. The eye moves from problem (outflows) → toolkit → buffer → early sign of success.
- **Sources:** RBI Governor’s Statement, 5 Jun 2026; A2Z Taxcorp, 5 Jun 2026; Candour Legal, 24 Jun 2026; Moneycontrol, 23 Jun 2026.

### Concept 3: The oil shock unwind (conditional)
- **Central insight:** A collapse in crude oil prices — down roughly **21%** in a month — is doing some of the RBI’s inflation-fighting work before the MPC has to lift rates.
- **Data to include:**
  - Brent crude **~$72.95/bbl** on 26 Jun 2026, down ~21% over the month.
  - RBI FY27 CPI projection **5.1%**, with Q3 at **5.9%** — only 10 bps below the 6% upper tolerance band.
  - Retail fuel prices raised **7.4%** (petrol) and **8.4%** (diesel) since May; RBI estimates direct inflation impact ~**36 bps**.
- **Visualization type:** Dual-track timeline / line chart.
- **Rough composition notes:** A steeply falling oil-price line dominates the upper half, annotated with the 18 Jun U.S.–Iran MoU and the 5 Jun RBI policy. Below it, a narrower band shows the RBI’s quarterly CPI projection path, with Q3 shaded close to the 6% ceiling. The payoff: lower oil buys time, but the margin is thin.
- **Sources:** Trading Economics, 27 Jun 2026; RBI MPC resolution, 5 Jun 2026.
- **Caveat:** The April ~$140/bbl peak cited in the synthesis is not independently sourced. Use only the **26 Jun price** and the **~21% monthly fall** unless a source for the peak is added.

### Concept 4: Rupee stabilised without a rate hike
- **Central insight:** Despite sustained foreign equity selling and a hawkish Fed dot plot, the rupee strengthened from **95.24** to **94.39** and India VIX fell below **13** — signs that capital-account tools and domestic flows absorbed the pressure.
- **Data to include:**
  - Net FPI outflows: **$13.7 billion** FY27 so far (till 2 Jun), mostly equity (**$13.4 bn**).
  - FII equity selling (30 days to 29 Jun): **−₹51,456.1 crore**.
  - DII equity buying (same 30 days): **+₹47,608.2 crore**.
  - Rupee: **95.24/USD** on 5 Jun → **94.39/USD** on 25 Jun.
  - India VIX: **12.76** on 25 Jun, below 13.
- **Visualization type:** Two-panel small multiple + outcome callouts.
- **Rough composition notes:** Split the story into two clearly labelled panels so the reader cannot compare a USD FY27-to-date series with an INR 30-day series on a single axis.
  - **Panel A — Capital-account pressure (FY27-to-date, USD):** A horizontal bar or waterfall showing net FPI outflows of **$13.7 bn**, split into equity (**$13.4 bn**) and debt (**$0.3 bn**). Label the period and currency explicitly: "FY27 so far, till 2 Jun 2026."
  - **Panel B — Domestic equity offset (30 days to 29 Jun, INR):** A side-by-side bar chart with two bars of clearly different colours: red for FII equity selling **−₹51,456 cr** and blue for DII equity buying **+₹47,608 cr**. Each bar has its own axis label in rupees; no attempt is made to sum or balance it against Panel A.
  - **Outcome callouts (separate from the two panels):** A small rupee badge showing **95.24 → 94.39/USD** and a "VIX 12.76" stability badge. Place them below the panels with a line such as "Rupee strengthened and volatility fell even as foreign portfolio money left."
  - The payoff: the RBI did not need to raise rates because capital-account measures, falling crude, and domestic buying each played a part — but the chart does not falsely equate dollars with rupees or a FY27-to-date window with a 30-day window.
- **Sources:** RBI Governor’s Statement, 5 Jun 2026; Trendlyne, 29 Jun 2026; HDFC SKY, 5 Jun and 25 Jun 2026.

## What to avoid

- **A simple world map of policy rates.** It would show levels but hide the divergence in forward paths and the different inflation-growth trade-offs each bank faces.
- **"RBI vs Fed" boxing-match framing.** Too reductive for a Layer 2 audience and implies a winner/loser prediction the research does not support.
- **The April ~$140/bbl Brent peak as a headline number** unless an independent source is added. The 26 Jun price and the ~21% monthly fall are already strong and verifiable.
- **Analyst estimates that FCNR(B) could bring in $40–50 billion.** These are speculative ranges, not data, and would overstate the case.
- **DXY >100 / EUR/USD <1.15 levels** without direct market-close sources. The research mentions them but does not provide the specific closes or attributions needed for an infographic.
- **Mixing FPI outflows ($) and FII/DII flows (₹)** without explicit period labels. The two series cover different units and different windows.

## Final recommendation

Proceed with **Concepts 1, 2, and 4** — they are the strongest and most completely sourced.

**Concept 3** is viable if the April Brent peak is either independently sourced or removed; in its current form the oil-price line should be limited to the 26 Jun level and the ~21% monthly decline.

Hold any concept built on the 2013 FCNR(B) $26–34 billion inflow comparison until a source for that figure is added to the research artifact.
