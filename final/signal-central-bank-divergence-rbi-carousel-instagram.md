# Instagram Promo Carousel — Central-Bank Divergence and the RBI's Capital-Account Optionality

**Signal ID:** central-bank-divergence-rbi  
**Format:** Instagram promo carousel (promotes the long-form essay)  
**Slide count:** 9

---

## Slide 1 — Hook

- **Kicker:** Policy anchor
- **Headline:** The global rate anchor came loose in mid-June.
- **Body:** For years the major central banks moved as a bloc — easing, tightening, pausing together. That coordination is gone.
- **Hero stat or quote:** Fed held at 3.50%–3.75%, ECB hiked to 2.25%, RBA paused at 4.35% — all within one week. (Federal Reserve, ECB, RBA, 11–17 Jun 2026)
- **Design note:** Minimal type on off-white. Three central-bank seals arranged diagonally; the Fed seal tilted slightly out of line. No other ornament.
- **Speaker note:** Names the shift: the world no longer has a single monetary anchor.

---

## Slide 2 — The Fed

- **Kicker:** The Fed
- **Headline:** The Fed did not hike — but its dot plot did.
- **Body:** The median 2026 funds-rate projection rose to 3.8%, up from 3.4% in March. Nine of eighteen FOMC members now see at least one hike this year.
- **Hero stat or quote:** Median 2026 fed funds projection: 3.8%, up from 3.4% in March. (Federal Reserve Summary of Economic Projections, 17 Jun 2026)
- **Design note:** A simple yield-curve line chart, front end lifting. Stark black and burnt-red palette; the number 3.8% is the only large type.
- **Speaker note:** Establishes that the Fed is hawkishly on hold.

---

## Slide 3 — Europe, Australia

- **Kicker:** Europe, Australia
- **Headline:** The ECB hiked into a downgrade; the RBA paused after three hikes.
- **Body:** Lagarde raised the deposit rate to 2.25% even as the ECB cut its 2026 GDP forecast to 0.8%. Australia, by contrast, stopped at 4.35%.
- **Hero stat or quote:** ECB deposit facility rate: 2.25%, first hike since Sep 2023; ECB 2026 GDP projection: 0.8%. (ECB, 11 Jun 2026)
- **Design note:** Split screen — ECB seal paired with a down arrow on growth, RBA seal paired with a flat line. Muted institutional colours.
- **Speaker note:** Shows divergence is real and asymmetric, not cosmetic.

---

## Slide 4 — Pivot (Thesis)

- **Kicker:** The thesis
- **Headline:** This isn't one global cycle. It's three local trade-offs that no longer add up.
- **Body:** Capital can no longer price against a single global rate path. It must now choose between currencies on forward expectations, not current levels.
- **Hero stat or quote:** "Currencies move on the forward path, not the current change." (Aagman Layer 2 Research)
- **Design note:** Large centred type on a near-black background. One phrase italicised: *three local trade-offs*. No imagery.
- **Speaker note:** States the thesis explicitly in the required "This isn't X. It's Y" form.

---

## Slide 5 — The Spillover

- **Kicker:** The spillover
- **Headline:** India faced the classic emerging-market squeeze.
- **Body:** A stronger dollar, higher U.S. front-end yields, and $13.7 billion of net FPI outflows so far this fiscal year. The textbook response is to raise rates.
- **Hero stat or quote:** Net FPI outflows in FY27 so far: $13.7 billion. (RBI Governor's Statement, 5 Jun 2026)
- **Design note:** Rupee symbol with a thin downward trend line; a faint dollar index rising in the background. Sparse, high contrast.
- **Speaker note:** Sets up the pressure the RBI chose not to meet with rate hikes.

---

## Slide 6 — RBI Response

- **Kicker:** RBI response
- **Headline:** The RBI kept the repo rate at 5.25% — and opened the capital-account toolbox.
- **Body:** The RBI's five measures target dollar inflows directly: wider FAR limits for G-Secs, higher NRI/OCI/PROI equity limits, concessional forex swaps for PSU ECBs, full hedging on fresh FCNR(B) deposits, and restored export-proceeds realisation to nine months. A separate government ordinance exempts FPIs and the BIS from tax on G-Sec interest and capital gains.
- **Hero stat or quote:** Repo rate: 5.25% (hold), stance: neutral; forex reserves: $682.3 billion. (RBI MPC resolution and Governor's Statement, 5 Jun 2026)
- **Design note:** A clean row of five simple icons or numbered blocks for the RBI levers, plus a smaller, differently coloured badge for the separate government ordinance. Monochrome with one accent colour.
- **Speaker note:** Introduces the alternative policy path.

---

## Slide 7 — The Mechanism

- **Kicker:** The mechanism
- **Headline:** Borrow dollars abroad, fund rupees at home — without touching the policy rate.
- **Body:** The FCNR(B) swap covers the full hedging cost; fresh deposits are exempt from CRR and SLR. Analysts estimate the window could pull in $40–50 billion, but actual mobilisation depends on bank pricing, relative spreads and global risk appetite.
- **Hero stat or quote:** FCNR(B) swap window: 8 Jun–30 Sep 2026; indicative USD rates 5.50%–7.10%. (RBI / Candour Legal, 24 Jun 2026)
- **Design note:** A flow diagram: dollar deposits → RBI swap → rupee liquidity. Thin arrows, plenty of negative space.
- **Speaker note:** Explains how the capital account substitutes for the rate channel.

- **Kicker:** Early results
- **Headline:** The rupee steadied, and foreign money returned to Indian bonds.

- **Hero stat or quote:** Rupee: 94.39/USD on 25 Jun 2026; FPI bond inflows: ₹35,000 crore as of 23 Jun 2026. (HDFC SKY, Moneycontrol)
- **Design note:** Two small charts side by side — rupee line rising left-to-right, bond inflow bar. Same accent colour as Slide 6.
- **Speaker note:** Shows the measures are already moving markets.

## Deck Arc Summary

The carousel opens by naming the break in the global monetary anchor (Slides 1–3), pivots to the thesis that this is three local trade-offs rather than one global cycle (Slide 4), and then turns to India's alternative path: absorbing external pressure through the capital account instead of raising rates (Slides 5–7). It closes with early market validation (Slide 8) and the open question that drives the full essay (Slide 9).
