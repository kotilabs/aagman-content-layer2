# LinkedIn Carousel — India VIX Compression

*Standalone carousel: full argument resolves within the deck.*

---

## Slide 1 — Hook

- **Kicker:** Volatility, compressed
- **Headline:** India VIX has fallen below 13 before the risks have gone away.
- **Body:** The gauge fell to an intraday low of 12.76 on 25 June. Over the preceding month, from late-May levels near 17–18, it had dropped roughly 35%. The move came while foreign selling, an RBI growth downgrade and an interim Middle East truce were all still unresolved.
- **Hero stat or quote:** India VIX intraday low 12.76 on 25 Jun 2026; 52-week range 8.86–28.91 — HDFC SKY; DealPlexus
- **Speaker note:** Names the shift: implied volatility is pricing calm even though the macro backdrop is still contingent.

---

## Slide 2 — The unwind

- **Kicker:** Two-session reversal
- **Headline:** The compression follows one of the sharpest geopolitical spikes of the year.
- **Body:** On 23 June, VIX jumped 8.57% to 13.94 as the Strait of Hormuz risk premium resurfaced. By the 25 June close it had fallen to 13.05.
- **Hero stat or quote:** India VIX 13.94 on 23 Jun → 13.05 on 25 Jun — Yahoo Finance ^INDIAVIX
- **Speaker note:** Establishes the speed of the reversal and the fragility of the new low.

---

## Slide 3 — Oil transmission

- **Kicker:** Supply shock, paused
- **Headline:** An interim US–Iran deal unwound the oil risk premium that had driven March volatility.
- **Body:** Brent fell to $72.4/bbl on 25 June, down from near $100 in May, as tanker traffic through Hormuz began to normalise. Lower crude reduces India’s inflation, current-account and balance-of-payments risk.
- **Hero stat or quote:** Brent crude $72.4/bbl on 25 Jun, down 1.8%; US–Iran interim deal signed 15 Jun — HDFC SKY; Angel One
- **Speaker note:** Identifies the first mechanical driver of the VIX fall.

---

## Slide 4 — RBI anchoring

- **Kicker:** Rates on hold
- **Headline:** The RBI’s neutral stance removed a near-term uncertainty from rate-sensitive stocks.
- **Body:** The MPC held the repo at 5.25% on 5 June and Governor Malhotra said on 25 June that a rate hike was not under consideration. Anchored short rates tend to compress volatility in banks, NBFCs and real estate.
- **Hero stat or quote:** Repo rate 5.25% unchanged; FY27 GDP forecast cut to 6.6%, CPI raised to 5.1% — RBI MPC resolution, 5 Jun 2026
- **Speaker note:** Adds the monetary-policy channel that has kept index-level implied volatility low.

---

## Slide 5 — Flow puzzle

- **Kicker:** Sellers, then buyers
- **Headline:** Foreign money has been leaving even as the index has held 24,000.
- **Body:** FIIs sold roughly ₹51,456 crore of equities over the 30 days to 29 June. DIIs bought consistently, including ₹5,747 crore on 25 June alone.
- **Hero stat or quote:** FII net sales ₹51,456.1 crore (30 days); DII net buying ₹5,747.75 crore on 25 Jun — Trendlyne; Kotak Neo
- **Speaker note:** Sets up the central tension — stability reflects domestic absorption, not consensus.

---

## Slide 6 — Pivot

- **Kicker:** Reframe
- **Headline:** This is not risk dissipating. It is risk being *priced away*.
- **Body:** Option buyers are paying less for protection because realised volatility has stayed low, not because the macro backdrop has become benign. The Nifty reclaimed 24,000 while foreign selling continued.
- **Hero stat or quote:** Nifty 50: 23,824.10 on 23 Jun → 24,056.00 on 25 Jun — Yahoo Finance ^NSEI
- **Speaker note:** Names the thesis explicitly — compression is not the same as resolution.

---

## Slide 7 — Complacency check

- **Kicker:** Little margin
- **Headline:** A VIX below 13 leaves scant premium for a deal collapse or an inflation surprise.
- **Body:** The RBI projects Q3 FY27 CPI at 5.9%, close to the 6% upper tolerance band, while the EIA still assumes Hormuz is “effectively closed” in the near term. One reading — Interpretation C from the research — is that the VIX is pricing complacency into a still-fragile macro environment. Present it as a plausible reading, not a conclusion: the oil unwind and domestic-flow absorption are equally part of the story.
- **Hero stat or quote:** RBI Q3 FY27 CPI forecast 5.9%; EIA assumes Hormuz effectively closed near-term, Brent $105/b in Jun–Jul — RBI; EIA June 2026 STEO
- **Speaker note:** Tests the thesis against the most concrete near-term vulnerabilities.

---

## Slide 8 — Cross-asset repricing

- **Kicker:** Not just equities
- **Headline:** If the VIX is wrong, the repricing will not stay in the stock market.
- **Body:** A hawkish RBI pivot or renewed oil pressure would simultaneously hit rate-sensitive sectors, credit spreads and the rupee. An under-hedged index leaves little protection if the anchor lifts.
- **Hero stat or quote:** "The central bank was not considering an interest rate hike at present" — RBI Governor Sanjay Malhotra, 25 Jun 2026, via HDFC SKY
- **Speaker note:** Extends the argument from volatility to broader asset prices.

---

## Slide 9 — Historical lens

- **Kicker:** Low and stable
- **Headline:** History says low VIX regimes can persist — but they do not forgive shocks.
- **Body:** The closest recent analogue is September 2025, when India VIX fell below 10 after the US Federal Reserve cut rates. Low VIX persisted for months, but short-term corrections were not ruled out. After VIX touched 10.13 in July 2023, Nifty rose 26.4% over one year. After April 2017’s low, it rose 11.5% over one year. Short-term drawdowns still occurred.
- **Hero stat or quote:** July 2023 VIX low: Nifty +26.42% over 1 year; April 2017 low: +11.52% — Ventura Securities
- **Speaker note:** Qualifies the contrarian case — low volatility is not automatically a sell signal.

---

## Slide 10 — Open question

- **Kicker:** What remains
- **Headline:** The question is whether domestic flows are masking a market that is under-hedged for the next 60 days.
- **Body:** The US–Iran negotiation window runs through mid-August. If the deal frays before foreign money returns, the VIX below 13 will look less like a verdict and more like a *position*.
- **Hero stat or quote:** US–Iran interim deal includes a 60-day negotiation extension from 15 Jun 2026 — Angel One
- **Speaker note:** Leaves the argument hanging on the key uncertainty.
