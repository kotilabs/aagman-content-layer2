## Slide 1 — The hook

**Kicker:** Policy anchor

**Headline:** In mid-June, the developed-world central-bank consensus finally came apart.

**Body:** For years the Fed, ECB and RBA moved in rough synchrony — easing together, tightening together, pausing together. In one week they stopped doing any of it.

**Hero stat/quote:** Nine of eighteen FOMC participants now project at least one rate hike by year-end 2026. — Federal Reserve, Summary of Economic Projections, 17 June 2026

**Design note:** Three central-bank logos or abbreviations, once aligned, now pulling in different directions.

**Speaker note:** Names the shift the essay will unpack — not the topic of central banking, but the end of the shared global anchor.

## Slide 2 — The Fed

**Kicker:** The Fed

**Headline:** The Fed held rates but lifted its 2026 dot from 3.4% to 3.8%.

**Body:** The median projection now implies at least one 25-basis-point hike this year. PCE inflation forecasts jumped to 3.6% from 2.7% — the largest single-meeting upward revision in years.

**Hero stat/quote:** Median 2026 fed funds rate projection: 3.8%, up from 3.4% in March. — Federal Reserve, Summary of Economic Projections, 17 June 2026

**Design note:** Fed dot plot chart with the March and June medians highlighted.

**Speaker note:** Shows the Fed is more hawkish than the "hold" headline suggests.

## Slide 3 — The ECB

**Kicker:** The ECB

**Headline:** The ECB hiked 25bp into a downgraded growth outlook.

**Body:** The first increase since September 2023 came as staff cut the 2026 GDP forecast to 0.8%. Lagarde called the decision unanimous and rejected the label "insurance hike."

**Hero stat/quote:** ECB deposit facility rate: 2.25%; 2026 euro-area GDP growth projection: 0.8%. — European Central Bank, "Monetary policy decisions," 11 June 2026

**Design note:** ECB rate decision juxtaposed with the downward GDP revision.

**Speaker note:** Demonstrates the ECB is fighting energy-driven inflation even as activity weakens.

## Slide 4 — The RBA

**Kicker:** The RBA

**Headline:** The RBA paused after three rapid hikes but kept a hiking bias.

**Body:** The cash rate stays at 4.35%. The board noted inflation is "still too high" and remains ready to tighten "further if required."

**Hero stat/quote:** RBA cash rate target: 4.35% (hold), after 25bp hikes in February, March and May 2026. — Reserve Bank of Australia, 16 June 2026

**Design note:** RBA statement pull-quote with the conditional hiking language underlined.

**Speaker note:** Adds the third divergence — a pause, not an end.

## Slide 5 — The pivot

**Kicker:** Thesis

**Headline:** This isn't one global cycle. It's three local trade-offs that no longer add up.

**Body:** The Fed worries about sticky services demand. The ECB reacts to an energy shock. The RBA watches domestic demand that may be cracking. Each bank is now solving its own equation — and capital must make relative judgments again.

**Hero stat/quote:** The dollar index broke above 100 and EUR/USD fell below 1.15 even after the ECB hiked. — Scotiabank, "Foreign Exchange Outlook," 19 June 2026

**Design note:** Three parallel equations, each with a different solution, diverging from a single line.

**Speaker note:** Names the essay's central argument in the required "This isn't X. It's Y" form.

## Slide 6 — The FX principle

**Kicker:** Forward path

**Headline:** Currencies price the expected future path, not today's decision.

**Body:** The ECB actually hiked; the dollar still strengthened. Markets compare where each central bank is *going*, not where it is. When the Fed's median dot rose, the relative return on dollar cash rose with it.

**Hero stat/quote:** DXY broke above 100; EUR/USD fell below 1.15 after the ECB hike. — Scotiabank, "Foreign Exchange Outlook," 19 June 2026

**Design note:** Two yield curves — U.S. and euro area — with the U.S. front end rising faster.

**Speaker note:** Explains the FX principle that makes divergence matter for emerging markets.

## Slide 7 — The RBI's choice

**Kicker:** RBI response

**Headline:** The RBI held the repo rate at 5.25% and chose a different kind of defence.

**Body:** Governor Malhotra judged the inflation impulse as supply-side — fuel pass-through, monsoon risk, global energy — and growth already slowing. The textbook rate hike would raise borrowing costs without lowering oil prices.

**Hero stat/quote:** RBI repo rate: 5.25% (hold); FY27 CPI inflation projection: 5.1%, with Q3 at 5.9%. — Reserve Bank of India, Monetary Policy Statement, 5 June 2026

**Design note:** RBI MPC vote tally or policy statement headline against an oil-price backdrop.

**Speaker note:** Explains why India rejected the emerging-market textbook of hiking to defend the currency.

---

## Slide 8 — The toolbox

**Kicker:** Capital account

**Headline:** Instead of raising rates, the RBI opened a capital-account toolbox.

**Body:** Five RBI measures: a wider Fully Accessible Route for G-Secs, higher NRI/OCI/PROI equity limits, concessional forex swaps for PSU external commercial borrowings, full hedging cost on fresh FCNR(B) deposits, and restored export-proceeds realisation to nine months. A separate government ordinance exempts FPIs and the BIS from tax on G-Sec interest and capital gains.

**Hero stat/quote:** Net FPI outflows in FY27 so far: $13.7 billion; forex reserves: $682.3 billion. — Reserve Bank of India, Governor's Statement, 5 June 2026

**Design note:** A literal five-item toolbox listing the RBI measures, with a separate government-ordinance callout.

**Speaker note:** Lists the RBI instruments, notes the separate government ordinance, and the external-account problem they are designed to solve.

**Hero stat/quote:** Rupee: 94.39/USD by 25 Jun 2026; FPI bond inflows: ₹35,000 crore; FII equity outflows: −₹51,456 crore. — HDFC SKY, Moneycontrol, Trendlyne

## Slide 10 — The open question + CTA

**Kicker:** Open question

**Headline:** If Q3 inflation prints near 6%, can the RBI really keep rates on hold?

**Body:** The neutral stance preserves optionality. But the FCNR(B) window is only four months, the yield spread over U.S. alternatives is thinner than in 2013, and the Fed's dots may still harden. The swap also transfers risk from banks to the RBI: it absorbs roughly 280–300bp of hedging cost and assumes currency risk over the deposit tenor. The essay asks whether this separation of domestic policy and external management can hold.

**Hero stat/quote:** FCNR(B) swap window: 8 June – 30 September 2026; RBI absorbs approximately 280–300bp of hedging cost. — Candour Legal / RBI Circular FMOD.MAOG.No.S-56, 8 June 2026

**Design note:** A scale balancing the repo rate against the capital-account tools, tilting uncertainly.
