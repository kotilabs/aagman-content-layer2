# Instagram Carousel Draft — Signal `india-vix-compression`

**Format:** Standalone Instagram carousel  
**Slide count:** 10  
**Voice:** Financial Times leader column meets Matt Levine — dry, confident, structurally argued, lightly literary.

---

## Slide 1 — Hook

**Kicker:** Volatility vanishes

**Headline:** India VIX has compressed below 13 before the risks have actually *cleared*.

**Body:** A month ago it was near 18. Two days earlier it spiked 8.57%. Now the options market is pricing calm.

**Hero stat:** 13.05 — India VIX close, 25 June 2026 (Yahoo Finance)

**Design note:** Large "13" as the hero number on a muted red-to-green gradient, with a thin descending line tracing VIX from ~18 to 13. Minimal text, high contrast.

**Speaker note:** Names the shift upfront — a volatility index falling through a psychological floor while the macro backdrop remains unresolved.

---

## Slide 2 — The spike and collapse

**Kicker:** Two-day reversal

**Headline:** VIX spiked 8.57% on 23 June, then fell below 13 by 25 June.

**Body:** A one-day jump in fear, followed by a near-instant collapse. Traders paid up for protection, then sold it back.

**Hero stat:** 13.94 (+8.57%) on 23 Jun; 13.05 on 25 Jun (Yahoo Finance)

**Design note:** Split screen — left half shows the spike candle in red, right half shows the compression in green. Stark black/white background with one bold number per side.

**Speaker note:** Establishes the speed of the reversal and frames the puzzle the deck will unpack.

---

## Slide 3 — The geopolitical unwind

**Kicker:** Oil relief

**Headline:** The US–Iran interim deal took the Hormuz risk premium out of the oil market.

**Body:** Brent fell from near $100 in May to $72.4 by 25 June. Lower crude reduces the risk to India's import bill and inflation outlook. The EIA still assumes Hormuz is “effectively closed” near term, with Brent averaging $105/b in June–July — so the risk premium could return quickly if the interim deal frays.

**Hero stat:** Brent $72.4/bbl, down 1.8% on 25 Jun (HDFC SKY)

**Design note:** A falling barrel chart or a silhouetted Strait of Hormuz map with the Brent price callout. Cool blue tones, sparse labels.

**Speaker note:** Introduces the first plausible explanation for the compression — a supply-shock premium unwinding.

---

## Slide 4 — The domestic wall

**Kicker:** Foreign out, domestic in

**Headline:** FIIs sold ₹51,456 crore in thirty days; DIIs were consistent net buyers.

**Body:** The "DII wall" cushioned the Nifty, held it above 23,750, and kept realised volatility low enough to drag implied volatility down.

**Hero stat:** FII equity net sales: ₹51,456.1 Cr over 30 days (Trendlyne)

**Design note:** Two opposing horizontal bars — FII red outflow on the left, DII green inflow on the right. Nifty anchored at 24,000 in the centre.

**Speaker note:** Introduces the flow mechanism that may be mechanically suppressing volatility regardless of the macro news.

---

## Slide 5 — The RBI anchor

**Kicker:** Rate hold

**Headline:** The RBI held at 5.25% and told the market it is not thinking about a hike.

**Body:** With the short rate anchored, rate-sensitive stocks stopped moving violently. That feeds directly into lower index volatility.

**Hero stat:** Repo rate 5.25% unchanged; Governor Malhotra: no hike under consideration (RBI / HDFC SKY)

**Design note:** A flat horizontal line at 5.25% against a timeline of MPC decisions. Institutional, almost austere.

**Speaker note:** Adds the policy channel that anchors the short end and dampens sectoral volatility.

---

## Slide 6 — Pivot

**Kicker:** The real story

**Headline:** The oil unwind helped. The *persistence* looks like a flow story.

**Body:** Oil de-risking, RBI anchoring and seasonal effects all contributed. But the compression kept going because domestic flows absorbed the bulk of foreign selling through June — including a strong DII buying day on 25 June even as FIIs turned marginally positive.

**Hero stat:** DII net buying: +₹5,747.75 Cr on 25 Jun alone (Kotak Neo / Trendlyne)

**Design note:** Bold typographic pivot — "Oil helped." on the top half, "Flows explain the persistence." on the bottom. Maximum contrast, no charts.

**Speaker note:** Names the thesis explicitly: oil is a real driver, but the structural explanation for the compression is domestic-flow absorption.

---

## Slide 7 — The macro tension

**Kicker:** Growth down, prices up

**Headline:** The RBI downgraded FY27 growth to 6.6% and raised inflation to 5.1%.

**Body:** Q3 CPI is projected at 5.9%, within sight of the 6% upper tolerance band. One reading — Interpretation C — is that the VIX is pricing complacency into a still-fragile macro environment. It is a plausible reading, not the conclusion: the oil unwind and the DII wall are also part of the story.

**Hero stat:** RBI FY27 GDP: 6.6% (down from 6.9%); CPI: 5.1% (up from 4.6%) (RBI MPC)

**Design note:** Two converging lines — GDP forecast drifting down, CPI forecast rising up. Muted warning tones, no grid clutter.

**Speaker note:** Names Interpretation C explicitly — the VIX may be pricing complacency into a still-fragile macro — while flagging it as one plausible reading alongside the oil and flow stories.

---

## Slide 8 — Seasonal and positioning effects

**Kicker:** June pattern

**Headline:** June has historically been a weak month for India VIX.

**Body:** The gauge has posted negative returns in eleven of the past eighteen years, averaging a 7.57% decline. Expiry on 25 June added one more reason to compress. The closest recent analogue is September 2025, when VIX dropped below 10 after a Fed cut — a reminder that low VIX can persist for months while carrying short-term vulnerability.

**Hero stat:** Negative June returns in 11 of past 18 years; avg decline 7.57% (HDFC SKY)

**Design note:** A calendar grid or seasonal heat map with June highlighted in a single accent colour. Clean and data-forward.

**Speaker note:** Acknowledges the mechanical/seasonal component so the argument does not overstate the signal.

---

## Slide 9 — The structural question

**Kicker:** Under-hedged regime

**Headline:** A VIX below 13 leaves little room if a *shock* arrives.

**Body:** Option protection is cheap because domestic flows are pinning the index. If those flows slow, the market may discover it has sold too much insurance.

**Hero stat:** 52-week range: 8.86 – 28.91 (DealPlexus)

**Design note:** A horizontal band showing the 52-week VIX range, with the current level marked close to the low. Sparse, ominous, one bold number.

**Speaker note:** Poses the central risk — under-hedging against a market structure dependent on one buyer class.

---

## Slide 10 — Open question

**Kicker:** What next

**Headline:** If FIIs keep selling at this pace, how long can the DII bid absorb them without a repricing?

**Body:** The next sixty days cover the US–Iran negotiation window and the August MPC. Calm is priced in. The argument is whether it has been earned.

**Hero stat:** US–Iran interim deal: 60-day negotiation extension (Angel One)

**Design note:** A single question as large typography, with a thin timeline bar marking 60 days and the August MPC. No clutter, no CTA, no link.

**Speaker note:** Leaves the reader with the unresolved tension between flow-driven stability and the macro/geopolitical risks the VIX is no longer pricing.

---

## Deck arc summary

The carousel opens by naming the shift — India VIX falling below 13 despite an unresolved macro and geopolitical backdrop. It then walks through the three plausible explanations (oil unwind, domestic flow absorption, RBI anchoring) before pivoting to the thesis that, while oil de-risking is a genuine driver, the persistence of the compression is better understood as a flow-driven structural phenomenon. A macro-tension slide and a seasonal-mechanics slide add nuance, and the deck closes by posing the open question: whether the DII bid can keep absorbing foreign selling through the US–Iran window and the August MPC without a volatility repricing.
