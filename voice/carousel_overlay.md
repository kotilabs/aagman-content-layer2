# Carousel Overlay

Carousels are discontinuous, scannable, and headline-driven. The reader supplies the connective tissue between slides.

## Universal rules

- Voice: Financial Times leader column meets Matt Levine — dry, confident, structurally argued, lightly literary.
- Lead with the concrete, follow with the abstract.
- Every slide should have one signal sentence — short, declarative, screenshot-quotable.
- Numbers carry the argument. Specific, sourced inline, never rounded into vagueness.
- If a slide has no number, it has a named mechanism (a permit, a quota, a tariff, a law).
- Use em dashes for pivots.
- Use italics sparingly — one conceptual word per slide, max.
- Cut transitional phrases ("Furthermore," "It's worth noting," "In today's world").
- No adjectives where a number will do. No massive, huge, explosive, game-changing.
- One pivot slide in the middle should name the thesis explicitly, often as "This isn't X. It's Y."
- Vary slide weight: dense evidence slides earn a sparse one-line slide next to them.

## Platform deltas

**LinkedIn**
- Slightly more formal. Headlines can be longer.
- Data callouts and named institutions land well.
- Last slide for promo: link to Substack with one sentence on the open question.
- Last slide for standalone: the open question, no external link.

**Instagram**
- More visual rhythm. Shorter headlines.
- Design notes matter more: what the image shows, color contrast, minimal text per slide.
- Last slide same as LinkedIn, but even more concise.

## Forbidden

- Rhetorical questions mid-deck
- "Imagine if…"
- "The future of X"
- Exclamation marks
- Emoji
- Hype openers ("You won't believe…")
- Engagement-bait ("Agree? 👇")

## Per-slide output

For each slide, provide:
- **Kicker** — 2-4 words above the headline
- **Headline** — the signal sentence
- **Body** — 1-3 supporting lines
- **Hero stat or quote** — with source
- **Design note** — what the visual should show
- **Speaker note** — what this slide does in the argument

## Length

- Promo: 7–11 slides.
- Standalone: 8–12 slides.
