## Independent gaps

- **Gap:** The infographic concepts never visualize the actual price-performance divergence that gives the signal its name.
  - Why it matters: The reader is told the metals are "diverging" but is never shown copper rising +10.5% YTD while gold has fallen from its Q1 open and from its January record. The divergence is the hook; without the price path, the infographic explains drivers without proving the phenomenon.
  - Suggested addition or correction: Add a small dual-axis price chart or paired YTD return bars (copper +10.5%, gold −8.6% from Q1 open) as an anchor panel, ideally Concept 1A or a header panel above Concept 1.
  - Severity: should-fix

- **Gap:** Gold's sharp correction from $5,501.70/oz to ~$4,010/oz is treated as context rather than a counterargument.
  - Why it matters: The signal frames gold as pricing "monetary risk," but a ~27% peak-to-trough drop in six months is also consistent with Interpretation D in the research: a fragile safe-haven premium that may have peaked. Ignoring this weakens the piece against skeptical readers.
  - Suggested addition or correction: In Concept 2 or a new concept, add a sentence or annotation: "Gold has corrected sharply from its Jan record even as central banks keep buying — the structural bid provides a floor, but the safe-haven premium is volatile."
  - Severity: should-fix

- **Gap:** The copper demand side is under-represented relative to the supply side.
  - Why it matters: The infographic leads with mine disruptions, negative TC/RCs, and tariffs, but the research emphasizes that Chinese and European industrial demand remains subdued and that ICSG/Goldman see a 2026 surplus. Without demand context, the reader may conclude copper is unambiguously tight.
  - Suggested addition or correction: Add a small panel or footnote on "Why surplus forecasts exist" — ICSG's 96k surplus, lower demand growth (1.6%), and higher scrap supply. This balances the supply-disruption panels.
  - Severity: should-fix

- **Gap:** The COMEX–LME spread narrowing to ~$200/tonne is mentioned but not interpreted.
  - Why it matters: The narrowing implies that arbitrage cargoes have partly eroded the U.S. tariff premium. If the infographic presents the $690 peak as a permanent structural distortion, it overstates the current state of the market.
  - Suggested addition or correction: In Concept 5, label the narrowing as "arbitrage cargoes arrived" and add a concluding note: "Spread remains elevated vs. history, but the extreme peak was temporary."
  - Severity: optional

- **Gap:** LME copper stocks fell from >390,000 tonnes in May to 284,175 tonnes in July even as forecasters project a 2026 surplus.
  - Why it matters: This tension — falling visible inventories vs. surplus forecasts — is one of the research artifact's most interesting contradictions and would make a strong visual.
  - Suggested addition or correction: Consider adding a small panel showing LME stock drawdown alongside the ICSG surplus forecast, with a caption explaining the possible explanations (tariff-driven inventory repositioning, unreported Chinese stocks, scrap supply).
  - Severity: optional

- **Gap:** Cross-asset implications are absent.
  - Why it matters: The research lists implications for mining equities, rates/FX, EM, credit, and aluminum substitution. The infographic is allowed to be narrower, but at least the copper-to-aluminum substitution point is relevant to the "physical constraints" thesis.
  - Suggested addition or correction: Optional side note in Concept 3 or Concept 5: high copper prices are increasing substitution incentives; this is a demand-side cap the supply bulls must overcome.
  - Severity: optional

## Verdict

Corrections required — 1 blocker and 6 should-fix items across 1 surface (infographic).

The infographic concepts are tightly sourced and generally faithful to the research artifact, but Concept 1's framing risks confirming the lazy-consensus "commodities rally" narrative instead of challenging it. The central headline and payoff line must be corrected so the visual clearly shows copper up and gold corrected in 2026. Once the blocker and the main should-fix items are addressed, the infographic will be ready for design execution and SEO/human review.
