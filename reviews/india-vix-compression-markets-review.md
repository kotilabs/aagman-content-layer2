## Independent gaps

- **Gap:** The Instagram deck does not flag the EIA's base-case assumption that Hormuz remains "effectively closed" in the near term.
  - Why it matters: The EIA assumption is the strongest evidence that the oil-risk premium could return even if the interim deal does not formally collapse. The LinkedIn deck includes it; Instagram omits it, weakening the contrarian case on the most visual surface.
  - Suggested addition or correction: Add a line in Slide 3 or Slide 7: "The EIA still assumes Hormuz is 'effectively closed' near term, with Brent averaging $105/b in June–July."
  - Severity: Optional

- **Gap:** Neither carousel uses the September 2025 analogue (VIX below 10 after the Fed cut) from the research.
  - Why it matters: The 2025 analogue is the closest recent episode and reinforces that low VIX can persist for months while carrying short-term vulnerability. Omitting it leaves the historical lens focused only on 2017/2023.
  - Suggested addition or correction: Add a one-line reference to the September 2025 episode in the historical/seasonal slide on either surface.
  - Severity: Optional

- **Gap:** Neither surface fully engages Interpretation C from the research — that the VIX may be pricing complacency into a still-fragile macro environment.
  - Why it matters: The decks lean into the "flow story / under-hedged" narrative. The RBI inflation/growth trade-off and global rate divergence are the strongest contrarian counter to the oil-unwind case; under-weighting them makes the piece read slightly more one-sided than the research.
  - Suggested addition or correction: In the macro-tension slide, explicitly name the interpretation: low VIX may reflect complacency, not just domestic-flow mechanics.
  - Severity: Should-fix
