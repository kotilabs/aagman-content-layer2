## Executive summary

The drafted surfaces are materially faithful to the research artifact and internally consistent on the headline numbers. The macro → markets → cross-asset logic holds up across the blog, thread, and LinkedIn carousel. The main risks are compression distortions in the Instagram carousel and a recurring ambiguity about the timing of FX moves relative to the ECB decision. No surface contains unsupported trading advice or a claim that directly contradicts the research artifact.

**Single most important correction:** Instagram Slide 3 must drop or rephrase the claim that the ECB "admitted it would miss its inflation target until late 2027." The ECB staff projections show headline inflation at 2.3% in 2027 and only reaching 2.0% in 2028; the correct framing is "above target through 2027" or "not expected to reach 2% until 2028."

## Independent gaps

- **Gap:** None of the surfaces notes that one FOMC participant projected a rate *cut* in 2026.
  - Why it matters: The 9-of-18 hike projections are the hawkish headline, but the lone cut projection shows the committee is genuinely split, not uniformly hawkish. Omitting it makes the Fed sound more monolithic than it is.
  - Suggested addition or correction: In the blog's Fed section and in LinkedIn/Instagram Slide 2, add: "One participant projected a cut, underscoring the committee divide." The thread could include it in Post 2 or 3.
  - Severity: [FYI]

- **Gap:** Absolute policy-rate levels are missing from all surfaces.
  - Why it matters: The ECB hiked to 2.25%, which is still 125bp+ below the Fed and more than 200bp below the RBA. The divergence is in direction and forward path, not just in levels, but readers may overstate how tight the ECB already is.
  - Suggested addition or correction: Add one sentence in the blog's three-bank table caption or in Instagram/LinkedIn Slide 1: "Even after the hike, the ECB's deposit rate at 2.25% remained the lowest of the three."
  - Severity: [FYI]

- **Gap:** The 60-day Iran negotiating window and implementation risks are underplayed outside the blog.
  - Why it matters: The research artifact lists Israeli objections, CIA skepticism, mine-clearing logistics, and the 60-day window as tail risks that could re-inflate the energy premium. The carousels mention "if the deal frays" but give no sense of the timeline or the specific fragility.
  - Suggested addition or correction: In Instagram/LinkedIn Slide 8 (oil complication), add: "The MoU starts a 60-day negotiating window; mines in Hormuz and Israeli objections could keep a residual premium."
  - Severity: [FYI]

- **Gap:** Credit spreads and emerging-market stress are in the blog but absent from carousels and thread.
  - Why it matters: This is appropriate compression for short-form surfaces, but the blog could strengthen its EM/credit section by noting that the stress depends on whether dollar strength is growth-driven or rates-driven — a nuance that is currently present but could be sharper.
  - Suggested addition or correction: No change required; the blog treatment is adequate.
  - Severity: [FYI]

- **Gap:** The RBA's domestic weakening (unemployment 4.5%, Q1 GDP 0.3% q/q) is mentioned in the blog but with numbers only in the interpretation section.
  - Why it matters: The RBA pause is easier to understand with the domestic data in hand. The carousels say "unemployment has risen and growth has slowed" without figures.
  - Suggested addition or correction: Add the numbers to Instagram/LinkedIn Slide 4: "Unemployment rose to 4.5% in April; Q1 GDP was just 0.3% q/q."
  - Severity: [FYI]
