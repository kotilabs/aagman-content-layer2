## Independent gaps

- **Gap:** The 2026 CRR/SLR exemption on fresh FCNR(B) deposits is underweighted in the main outputs.
  - Why it matters: The exemption is a key reason banks can offer attractive FCNR(B) rates without hurting their economics; omitting it makes the mechanism look like pure RBI subsidy rather than a combined rate + regulatory incentive.
  - Suggested addition or correction: Add one sentence in the blog mechanism section and a bullet in the LinkedIn/Instagram toolbox slides noting that fresh FCNR(B) deposits under the window are exempt from CRR/SLR.
  - Severity: should-fix.

- **Gap:** The falling real policy rate is not made explicit outside the blog’s Reading two.
  - Why it matters: With CPI projected at 5.1% and the repo held at 5.25%, the ex-ante real rate is thin and could turn negative if inflation surprises higher. This is the core risk to the RBI’s optionality.
  - Suggested addition or correction: Add one thread post and one carousel slide noting that a hold at 5.25% with rising CPI means the real rate is compressing, which could force a hike if Q3 prints near 6%.
  - Severity: optional.

- **Gap:** The legal/political durability of the retrospective G-Sec tax exemption is not discussed.
  - Why it matters: Retrospective tax exemptions via ordinance can be challenged or reversed in Parliament; the permanence of the bond-inflow incentive affects the durability of the signal.
  - Suggested addition or correction: Add a one-line caveat in the blog and a small note in the carousel toolbox slide that the exemption is an ordinance and may face legislative or judicial review.
  - Severity: optional.
