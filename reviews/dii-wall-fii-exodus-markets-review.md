## Executive summary

The infographic concepts are visually strong and correctly surface the record DII absorption / FII exit story. However, the highest-impact concept (Concept 1) mixes two time windows in a way that contradicts its own headline, and the set as a whole leans into the structural-domesticization narrative without giving the reader any visual cue that the opposite interpretations (cyclical risk-off, valuation reset, RBI policy choice) are also live. A few word choices and visual-logic labels need tightening before production. Independent spot-checks confirm the headline numbers are broadly accurate, but FII outflow estimates vary materially by source/segment, so the infographic should not present one estimate as the single official figure.

## Independent gaps

- **Gap:** The infographic concepts do not engage the strongest counterargument to the structural-domesticization thesis: SIP stoppage ratios crossed 100% in March and April 2026, and equity-MF inflows fell 40% month-on-month in May.
  - Why it matters: Without this context, Concept 4 ("The Automatic Bid") risks telling readers that domestic flows are mechanically unstoppable. The research explicitly flags this as evidence that could contradict the structural story.
  - Suggested addition or correction: Add a small annotation, secondary bar, or footnote on Concept 4: "Rupee inflows held above ₹30k cr, but account-level SIP exits exceeded starts in Mar–Apr and equity-MF inflows dropped 40% in May."
  - Severity: [MAJOR]

- **Gap:** No concept represents the competing interpretations in research §5 (cyclical global risk-off, valuation reset after India premium, RBI choosing growth over currency credibility).
  - Why it matters: Infographics compress nuance; a one-sided structural story can look like consensus cheerleading. The research itself labels the structural interpretation as contested.
  - Suggested addition or correction: Add a "Three ways to read the flip" mini-concept or caveat panel with one-line visual cues for structural, cyclical, and valuation interpretations.
  - Severity: [MAJOR]

- **Gap:** The concepts do not show sector-level concentration of foreign selling (financials ~51% of March FII selling) or the divergence between large-cap financials/IT and domestic-themed mid/small-caps.
  - Why it matters: This is the mechanism behind Concept 3 ("Split Market"). A sector lens would make the large-cap vs. mid/small-cap divergence more concrete.
  - Suggested addition or correction: If precise monthly sector-flow data are available, add a small panel to Concept 3 showing FII selling concentrated in financials/IT and DII additions in private banks/technology/telecom/real estate/healthcare/NBFCs.
  - Severity: [FYI]

- **Gap:** The role of DII buying funded by debt-to-equity reallocation or cash deployment is not addressed.
  - Why it matters: Not all DII buying need be fresh household savings; some could be institutional reallocation. The mechanism in research §4 assumes rupee inflows that "must be deployed," but does not rule out reallocation.
  - Suggested addition or correction: Add a one-line caveat in Concept 4 or Concept 1: "DII buying reflects both fresh SIP/pension/insurance flows and institutional deployment of existing liquidity."
  - Severity: [FYI]
