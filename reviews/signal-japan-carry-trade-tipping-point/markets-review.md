## Independent gaps

- **Gap:** The geopolitical / energy-driven interpretation of yen weakness is absent from both surfaces.
  - Why it matters: The research artifact lists it as Interpretation C and notes that Japan imports nearly all its energy and that Middle East conflict has raised oil prices and widened the trade deficit. Ignoring this channel makes the content look one-dimensional and consensus-aligned ("it's all rates").
  - Suggested addition or correction: Add one paragraph in the blog's interpretation spectrum and a one-line caveat in the infographic's "closing spread" concept noting that energy/geopolitics can override or delay the rate-differential story.
  - Severity: should-fix

- **Gap:** The IMF July 2026 World Economic Outlook projection for Japan (0.6% real GDP growth in 2026, 0.7% in 2027) is not used.
  - Why it matters: It helps readers assess whether the BoJ can keep hiking into anemic growth, and it supports the "speed limit, not ceiling" framing.
  - Suggested addition or correction: Add one sentence in the blog's base-case or fiscal-constraint paragraph: "The IMF's July 2026 update projects Japan's real GDP growth at only 0.6% in 2026, leaving limited room for the BoJ to tighten aggressively without tightening into stagnation."
  - Severity: optional

- **Gap:** Governor Ueda's absence from the June 2026 decision is not surfaced.
  - Why it matters: A 7–1 vote without the sitting governor raises communication and leadership-continuity questions that are relevant to how markets interpret the hiking guidance.
  - Suggested addition or correction: Note Ueda's absence in the Context section.
  - Severity: optional

- **Gap:** The blog's cross-asset implications are asserted rather than argued.
  - Why it matters: The piece is aimed at decision-makers; simply listing asset-class exposures is less useful than showing the transmission mechanism (e.g., JPY appreciation → mark-to-market losses → forced liquidation of liquid winners → equity volatility).
  - Suggested addition or correction: Pick the two highest-consequence channels (US equities/Nasdaq and JGBs/fiscal) and expand each by two sentences walking through the mechanism.
  - Severity: optional
