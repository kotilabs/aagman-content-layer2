**Signal ID:** china-slower-growth

**Review date:** 2026-06-27  
**Reviewer:** Aagman Layer 2 Markets Reviewer

---

## Executive summary

Both carousels are structurally sound and track the research artifact closely. The core numbers are accurate and the two-speed narrative is well supported. However, two [BLOCKER]-level issues must be fixed before approval: (1) the central theses of the two carousels diverge materially, and (2) the Instagram carousel implies the PBOC has already delivered broad rate/RRR cuts, which the research artifact does not support. Several [MAJOR] and [MINOR] issues around omitted counter-signals, stale forecasts, and interpretive overstatement also need attention.

## Independent gaps

- **Gap:** Neither carousel surfaces the May 2026 counter-signals that complicate the slowdown narrative: the urban surveyed unemployment rate fell to 5.1% from 5.2%, and new total social financing flow rebounded sharply to CNY 2.03 trillion in May from CNY 620 billion in April.
  - Why it matters: A rigorous macro signal must acknowledge data that weakens or qualifies the dominant thesis. Omitting these makes the slowdown look more one-sided than the research artifact allows.
  - Suggested addition or correction: Add a one-line kicker in the credit/Beijing slide of each carousel: "May TSF flow rebounded to CNY 2.03T, but stock growth and loan demand remain weak." Add a labour-market note: "The unemployment rate ticked down to 5.1%, so the weakness is showing up in balance sheets and prices before jobs."
  - Severity: [MAJOR]

- **Gap:** The first-tier city property stabilisation is underweighted. National prices are falling, but first-tier new home prices rose 0.2% m/m in May.
  - Why it matters: Ignoring the first-tier stabilisation risks overstating the uniformity of the property collapse and opens the carousel to charges of cherry-picking.
  - Suggested addition or correction: In the property slide of each carousel, add one line: "First-tier cities show tentative stabilisation; lower-tier cities are still falling."
  - Severity: [MINOR]

- **Gap:** Both carousels omit the cross-asset read-throughs in the research artifact: Shanghai Composite at 4,027 (down 1.62% over the month), USD/CNY at 6.80, and the 10-year CGB yield at 1.73%.
  - Why it matters: The signal's value includes cross-asset implications. The two-speed economy has clear asset-price expressions that would strengthen the piece.
  - Suggested addition or correction: Add a one-line cross-asset kicker in one slide per carousel: "Bond yields are pricing weak growth; the yuan is stable on the record surplus."
  - Severity: [MINOR]

- **Gap:** The research artifact and both carousels rely on data dated June 2026, which cannot be independently verified from current external sources.
  - Why it matters: The Markets Reviewer cannot fully discharge the factual-integrity pass when all time-sensitive claims are forward-dated.
  - Suggested addition or correction: In production, re-verify every number against the stated sources immediately before publication. If publication slips beyond the research date, flag the entire signal for a source re-check.
  - Severity: [MAJOR]
