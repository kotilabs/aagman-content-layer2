## Independent gaps

- **Gap:** No concept visualizes the CPI/PPI split, which the research flags as a key mechanism showing weak final demand versus upstream cost-push pressure.
  - Why it matters: The gap between CPI (+1.2% y/y, core +1.1%) and PPI (103.8, rising) is a clean, intuitive way to illustrate the two-speed economy and downstream margin squeeze. It would strengthen Concept 1 or stand alone.
  - Suggested addition or correction: Add a small panel or separate concept showing CPI/core CPI versus PPI, with the interpretation that consumer demand is soft while producer prices are driven by energy/commodity costs.
  - Severity: should-fix

- **Gap:** The downward unemployment tick (5.1% in May, from 5.2% in April) is omitted entirely.
  - Why it matters: It is the main counterpoint to the domestic-demand-recession narrative and appears in the research's "evidence contradicting" Interpretation A. Omitting it risks one-sidedness.
  - Suggested addition or correction: Include the unemployment figure as a small counter-indicator in Concept 1 or the verified-data table, with context that the labour-market headline is stable despite soft confidence.
  - Severity: optional

- **Gap:** Cross-asset implications (CGB yields, CNY stability, sectoral equity dispersion) are not visualized.
  - Why it matters: The research devotes a full section to cross-asset implications; an infographic audience could benefit from the rates/FX/equity read-through.
  - Suggested addition or correction: Optional sixth concept or extension to Concept 5 showing 10Y CGB yield (1.73%), USD/CNY (6.80), and the equity domestic-vs-export split.
  - Severity: optional
