## Independent gaps

- **Gap:** The 8 July 2026 VIX spike is under-weighted in the blog and only appears in one infographic concept.
  - Why it matters: The spike occurred on the same day the research was completed. It directly challenges any "VIX calm = foreign-selling-contained" narrative and shows the calm is fragile. Ignoring it risks looking stale within days of publication.
  - Suggested addition or correction: Add the spike to the blog's "Signal / Deviation" section and ensure any final infographic based on Concept 3 includes the inset warning.
  - Severity: should-fix

- **Gap:** Sectoral ownership and flow dynamics are almost entirely absent.
  - Why it matters: The research artifact explicitly notes IT, financials and FMCG saw the largest FII stake cuts, while DIIs bought defensives, quality private banks and domestic themes. Without this, the blog's claim that "large-cap, liquid names remain vulnerable" is generic.
  - Suggested addition or correction: Add a paragraph or table showing which sectors are experiencing the largest FII cuts and DII buying.
  - Severity: optional

- **Gap:** The "end-July" HSBC positioning data is internally inconsistent with the artifact completion date.
  - Why it matters: The artifact is dated 8 July 2026 but cites data "by end-July". This propagates to both blog and infographic. If the date is wrong, it undermines the contrarian positioning argument; if it is a forecast/cited estimate, it should be labeled as such.
  - Suggested addition or correction: Verify the HSBC note date and whether "end-July" is a typo, a forward estimate, or a trailing window. Label accordingly.
  - Severity: should-fix

- **Gap:** The divergence between falling equity-MF inflows and steady SIP contributions is under-explored.
  - Why it matters: Equity MF net inflows fell 40.4% MoM in May 2026, yet SIP rupee contributions stayed above ₹30,000 crore. This implies lump-sum/direct buying weakened faster than the SIP machine, a nuance that strengthens the "account-level breadth may be narrowing" point.
  - Suggested addition or correction: In the blog's SIP warning, explicitly contrast the 40.4% equity-MF inflow drop with the stable SIP contribution.
  - Severity: optional

- **Gap:** The external sector / BoP financing constraint is presented without the RBI's own framing.
  - Why it matters: The RBI Annual Report explicitly said portfolio flows were "short for financing CAD." That is a stronger, more authoritative framing than the blog's generic "current account deficit must be financed."
  - Suggested addition or correction: Quote or paraphrase the RBI's financing-shortfall language to add authority to the external-loop discussion.
  - Severity: optional

- **Gap:** Oil and commodity dynamics are missing from the blog despite being in the research artifact.
  - Why it matters: Brent crude above $100 in May 2026 and the rupee's weakness are linked to the external loop. Omitting oil makes the currency discussion seem purely financial-flow driven.
  - Suggested addition or correction: Add one sentence noting the West Asia conflict's impact on oil and the feedback to India's CAD/inflation/currency.
  - Severity: optional
