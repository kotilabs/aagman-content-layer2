## Independent gaps

- **Gap:** The outputs do not mention that May 2026 payrolls were strong (+172,000 initially, revised to +129,000) and that unemployment held at 4.3% in May.
  - Why it matters: The "labor market cracked" framing in the carousels and thread makes the June slowdown look more abrupt than it was. May strength is important context for whether June is noise or a trend break.
  - Suggested addition or correction: Add one sentence in the blog and thread noting the May payroll strength and the subsequent revisions.
  - Severity: should-fix.

- **Gap:** TIPS and breakeven inflation data are missing, even though they would reinforce the core thesis.
  - Why it matters: If nominal yields are rising while inflation breakevens are stable/soft, the move is more clearly a real-yield / term-premium story. This would also head off the thread's incorrect "inflation expectations" framing.
  - Suggested addition or correction: Add a brief note or table in the blog (and optionally one carousel slide) citing 10-year TIPS yields and breakeven inflation from the Fed's H.15 release.
  - Severity: should-fix.

- **Gap:** The CME FedWatch path (11% July hike, 59% October hike, 96.3% December hike) is compressed into the December number only.
  - Why it matters: Using only the 96.3% December figure can overstate near-term hike certainty.
  - Suggested addition or correction: Where space permits, include the July and October probabilities alongside the December number.
  - Severity: optional.

- **Gap:** The analysis relies on a single term-premium model (San Francisco Fed CR model) without acknowledging model uncertainty.
  - Why it matters: Term-premium estimates are not directly observable and can vary materially across models (e.g., NY Fed ACM, Kim-Wright). Resting the thesis on one estimate creates false precision.
  - Suggested addition or correction: Add a one-line caveat that term-premium estimates are model-dependent.
  - Severity: optional.

## Verdict

**Corrections required — 2 blockers across 4 surfaces, plus 4 should-fix items.**

The two blockers are: (1) the repeated misstatement of the unemployment rate direction, and (2) the thread's unsupported attribution of the steepening to "inflation expectations." Both need to be fixed before approval. The remaining should-fix items are credibility and nuance issues that can be addressed quickly.
