## Independent gaps

- **Gap:** The concepts do not proactively visualize the core asymmetry thesis — that the risk premium could rebuild quickly if the 60-day talks stall.
  - Why it matters: The research artifact's signal restatement is built on this asymmetry ("if the 60-day talks stall, demining is delayed, or regional actors object, the risk premium could rebuild quickly"). The "What to avoid" section correctly rejects an "everything is fine" framing, but no concept explicitly shows the upside-tension scenario.
  - Suggested addition or correction: Add a small asymmetry panel to Concept 1 or Concept 2 showing the 60-day window against an illustrative tail-risk path (e.g., stalled talks → demining delays → shipping premiums re-price). Keep it clearly labeled as illustrative, not predictive.
  - Severity: optional

- **Gap:** The 30-day blockade-removal deadline and the 60-day negotiation window are mentioned together but could be visually conflated.
  - Why it matters: The 30-day clause is a near-term implementation milestone; the 60-day window is the broader diplomatic deadline. The research emphasizes the interim nature of the MOU, and confusing the two timelines weakens that point.
  - Suggested addition or correction: In Concept 1, use distinct colors, icons, or lane labels for the 30-day blockade-removal track and the 60-day negotiation track.
  - Severity: optional

- **Gap:** Concept 5 mentions the Fed/rates driver but does not surface Interpretation C from the research as a competing explanation.
  - Why it matters: The research presents "dollar and real rates, not Iran" as a valid competing interpretation. Acknowledging it in a small footnote or callout strengthens the infographic's analytical credibility and avoids a monocausal gold narrative.
  - Suggested addition or correction: Add a micro-callout in Concept 5: "Rates/dollar channel also bearish; late-June correlation with Iran news suggests war premium is the marginal driver."
  - Severity: optional
