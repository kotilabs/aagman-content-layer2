## Recommended changes

1. **Location:** First 100 words, after subheadline.
   - **Issue:** Direct structural takeaway is implied.
   - **Fix:** Add: "The risk is not the next rate hike; it is the mechanical feedback loop that can force leveraged yen-funded positions to close all at once."
   - **Priority:** High

2. **Location:** Top of the article.
   - **Issue:** Missing definition pattern.
   - **Fix:** Add after the opening analogy: "A yen carry trade is a leveraged position that borrows cheap yen and invests in higher-yielding assets elsewhere. It works until the yen rises fast enough to force repayment."
   - **Priority:** High

3. **Location:** H2s in the body.
   - **Issue:** Structural headings are not query-matched.
   - **Fix:** Add a new H2 "What is the yen carry trade?" before the mechanism section, or retitle "Signal / Deviation" to "Why is the yen still weak if the BoJ is hiking?"
   - **Priority:** High

4. **Location:** Body and conclusion.
   - **Issue:** No internal links or explicit bias flag.
   - **Fix:** Add 3–5 internal links and one sentence declaring Aagman's structural bias on Japan as a developed-economy weak link.
   - **Priority:** Medium

5. **Location:** Front matter / publishing metadata.
   - **Issue:** Missing URL slug and meta description.
   - **Fix:** Use slug `/japan-yen-carry-trade-tipping-point-2026` and meta description: "The Bank of Japan has hiked to 1.0% and is guiding for more, yet the yen is near a 40-year low. We explain the carry-trade feedback loop and what could unwind it."
   - **Priority:** Quick Win
