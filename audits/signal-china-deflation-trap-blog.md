## Executive summary

The draft is fact-dense, structurally sound, and respects uncertainty. Its biggest SEO/AEO opportunity is to make the first 100 words more directly answerable and to add question-based H2s that match how readers (and AI engines) ask about China's stimulus-demand gap. A few internal links and a clearer meta description would complete the optimization without bloating the piece.

## Recommended changes

1. **Location:** First 100 words, after subheadline.
   - **Issue:** Direct structural takeaway is implied but not stated.
   - **Fix:** Add one sentence: "The gap between supply-side stimulus and household balance-sheet repair is the signal to watch."
   - **Priority:** High

2. **Location:** H2s in the body.
   - **Issue:** Headings are structural, not query-matched.
   - **Fix:** Retitle "Mechanism" to "Why China's stimulus is not reaching household demand" or add a new H2 "Why isn't China's stimulus creating inflation?" before the mechanism section.
   - **Priority:** High

3. **Location:** Top of the article.
   - **Issue:** Missing definition pattern for answer engines.
   - **Fix:** Add a one-sentence definition after the opening analogy: "A deflation trap, here, means stimulus large enough to hit growth targets but too supply-side to lift prices while households repair balance sheets."
   - **Priority:** Medium

4. **Location:** Body and conclusion.
   - **Issue:** No internal links or explicit bias flag.
   - **Fix:** Add 3–5 internal links (Japan carry trade, India manufacturing, global rate-cut cycle, commodity supercycle) and one sentence declaring Aagman's structural bias on overcapacity/export deflation.
   - **Priority:** Medium

5. **Location:** Front matter / publishing metadata.
   - **Issue:** Missing URL slug and meta description.
   - **Fix:** Use slug `/china-deflation-trap-stimulus-without-demand-2025` and meta description: "China hit its 5% GDP target in 2025 while CPI was flat and producer prices fell. We break down why stimulus is flowing to factories, not households, and what it means for global markets."
   - **Priority:** Quick Win

## What to leave alone

- The editor's note is a strong trust signal and should not be removed.
- The interpretation spectrum is well balanced; do not collapse it into a single narrative.
- The restaurant analogy is within the 100–150 word target and sets the right conversational tone.
- Factual claims and source attributions are already strong.
