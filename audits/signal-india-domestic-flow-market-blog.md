## Executive summary

The draft is structurally sound, factually dense, and faithful to the Layer 2 voice. Its strongest asset is the ownership-crossover thesis, supported by multiple data tables and a clean interpretation spectrum. The single most important optimization is to restructure the front matter so the H1 and first 100 words answer a searchable question directly—moving the analogy from the lead to a supporting role—while adding 3–5 descriptive internal links and a source table for charts/data.

## Recommended changes

- **Location:** Title / H1
  - **Issue:** H1 is poetic but not query-aligned; the real SEO headline is currently the H2 subtitle.
  - **Fix:** Change H1 to **"India’s Domestic Flows Are Absorbing the Biggest Foreign Selloff in Years"**. Move "When the Locals Become the Market" to a deck/subtitle or pull quote.
  - **Priority:** Critical

- **Location:** URL / front matter
  - **Issue:** No slug or meta description provided.
  - **Fix:** Use slug `/india-domestic-flows-absorb-record-fpi-selling-2026`. Add meta description: **"Foreign investors have pulled record sums from Indian equities in 2026, yet domestic institutional flows have absorbed the selling and pushed DII ownership above FPI ownership for the first time in years."**
  - **Priority:** Critical

- **Location:** Opening paragraph / first 100 words
  - **Issue:** The direct structural answer is delayed until the "Context" section.
  - **Fix:** After the analogy, add one plain sentence: **"India’s equity market is experiencing a historic ownership crossover: foreign portfolio investors have sold record amounts in 2026, while domestic institutional investors—mutual funds, insurers and SIP flows—have absorbed enough of that selling to become larger owners than foreigners for the first time in over a decade."**
  - **Priority:** High

- **Location:** H2 subheadings
  - **Issue:** Several subheads are internal labels, not search queries.
  - **Fix:** Rephrase as follows:
    - "Signal / Deviation: What Is Unusual?" → **"Why hasn’t the Nifty crashed despite record FPI selling?"**
    - "Interpretation Spectrum: Four Plausible Readings" → **"What are the four plausible readings of this signal?"**
    - "Historical / Structural Memory: Two Useful Analogues" → **"What can the 2013 Taper Tantrum and 2020 crash teach us?"**
  - **Priority:** High

- **Location:** Body / first table
  - **Issue:** Core concept is not defined in an AEO-friendly "X is..." pattern.
  - **Fix:** Add a definition sentence before Table 1: **"The India domestic-flow market shift is the transition where domestic institutional investors, led by mutual fund SIPs and insurance flows, replace foreign portfolio investors as the marginal owners and price-setters of Indian equities."**
  - **Priority:** Medium

- **Location:** Internal links
  - **Issue:** No internal links to other Aagman content.
  - **Fix:** Add 3–5 descriptive internal links on: SIPs, MSCI EM / emerging-market flows, 2013 Taper Tantrum, India VIX, RBI policy cycle.
  - **Priority:** High

  - **Issue:** Aagman’s structural bias is not explicitly flagged.
  - **Fix:** Add one sentence: **"Aagman’s structural bias is that India is in a long-term domestic-demand-driven bull market; here that bias is an undercurrent, not a conclusion, and the data on SIP stoppage ratios and persistent FPI selling are treated as counter-evidence."**
  - **Priority:** Medium

- **Location:** Visual / chart opportunity
  - **Issue:** Tables are strong, but a chart would reinforce the crossover claim and improve rich-result potential.
  - **Fix:** Add a placeholder chart: **"FPI vs DII ownership share of NSE-listed companies, 2012–2026"** with source, timeframe, and units labeled.
  - **Priority:** Medium

## What to leave alone

- The Layer 2 canonical structure (Context → Mechanism → Signal → Interpretation → Historical Memory → Implications → Open Question) is intact and should not be reordered.
- The analogy opening is well within the 100–150-word target and preserves the conversational Layer 2 voice; do not remove it, only add a direct answer after it.
- The four-interpretation spectrum and the explicit "this is not a recommendation" framing are strengths; preserve both.
- The factual claims, numbers, and source list are accurate and traceable to the research artifact; do not alter verified data for SEO purposes.
- The open-ending question is strong and should remain unchanged.
