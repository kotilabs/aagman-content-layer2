## Executive summary

The draft clearly establishes a second-order signal — the AI bottleneck has moved from GPUs to power electronics and grid infrastructure. SEO/AEO improvements are mostly about making the article more discoverable for readers asking *why* the AI buildout is stalling and *what* the downstream bottlenecks are. Add question-based headings, internal links, and a concise definition up front.

## Recommended changes

1. **Location:** First 100 words, after subheadline.
   - **Issue:** Direct structural takeaway is implied.
   - **Fix:** Add: "The binding constraint has shifted from the chip everyone talks about to the power electronics almost nobody noticed."
   - **Priority:** High

2. **Location:** Top of the article.
   - **Issue:** Missing definition pattern.
   - **Fix:** Add after the opening analogy: "The power-semiconductor wall is the point where AI data-center buildouts are gated not by GPU or HBM supply, but by mature-node power chips, transformers, and grid interconnection."
   - **Priority:** High

3. **Location:** H2s in the body.
   - **Issue:** Structural headings are not query-matched.
   - **Fix:** Retitle "Context: What Changed?" to "Why are AI data centers hitting a power wall?" or add a new question H2 before "Mechanism."
   - **Priority:** High

4. **Location:** Body and conclusion.
   - **Issue:** No internal links or explicit bias flag.
   - **Fix:** Add 3–5 internal links and one sentence declaring Aagman's AI-as-capital-shock bias.
   - **Priority:** Medium

5. **Location:** Front matter / publishing metadata.
   - **Issue:** Missing URL slug and meta description.
   - **Fix:** Use slug `/ai-data-center-power-semiconductor-wall-2026` and meta description: "AI data centers are no longer gated by GPUs. Power management chips, transformers, and grid interconnection are the new bottlenecks. We explain the mechanism and what it means."
   - **Priority:** Quick Win
